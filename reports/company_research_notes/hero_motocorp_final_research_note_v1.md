## 1. Final investment-style conclusion

Hero MotoCorp is a high-quality Indian two-wheeler franchise with strong brand equity, mass-market leadership, broad distribution, low leverage, strong ROCE, good dividend payout, and improving operating momentum from rural demand, scooters, exports, premium motorcycles, and VIDA EV growth.

However, based on the reconciled first-version DCF and current market price proxy of approximately Rs 5,288 per share, the stock does not qualify as a base-case margin-of-safety candidate under a 25% MOS requirement.

### Final classification v1

> Quality company / Watchlist candidate — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. A better entry would require either a lower market price or stronger evidence that premium, scooter, EV, and export growth can sustainably lift FCFF above the base-case assumptions.

### Final valuation output v1

| Scenario | Intrinsic value per share | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Bear | ~Rs 2,707 | Downside case if growth/margins weaken and WACC rises |
| Base | ~Rs 4,170 | Below current price; no 25% MOS |
| Bull | ~Rs 6,041 | Upside case if premiumisation, scooters, exports, and VIDA execute well |

Current price proxy used: Rs 5,288 per share.

Base-case 25% MOS buy-zone price: ~Rs 3,128 per share.

## 3. Business overview

Hero MotoCorp is one of India's largest two-wheeler manufacturers and has historically dominated the commuter motorcycle segment. The company is built on:

1. Mass-market motorcycle leadership
2. Strong rural and semi-urban customer base
3. Large dealer and service network
4. High replacement demand
5. Low financial leverage
6. Strong operating cash generation
7. Strategic movement into premium motorcycles, scooters, exports, and EVs

Hero's historical strength is not only product manufacturing but distribution reach, after-sales service, trust, and affordability. These matter particularly in rural and entry-level motorcycle markets.

Interpretation:

- Q3 FY26 confirms strong near-term momentum.
- Revenue growth is being supported by domestic and export demand.
- Margins remain around the 14%-15% zone, supporting the base-case DCF.

### FY26 dispatch update

For March 2026, Hero reported 598,198 dispatches, up from 549,604 units in March 2025. Domestic sales were 552,505 units and exports were 45,693 units. FY26 dispatches were around 6.47 million units, implying about 10% volume growth over FY25. VIDA EV registrations and dispatches showed strong growth during FY26.

Interpretation:

- FY26 volume momentum is better than the historical 5-year sales CAGR.
- The recovery supports a higher near-term growth assumption.
- The base case still fades growth over time because the core commuter motorcycle market is mature.

## 6. Growth-driver analysis

### 6.1 Core commuter motorcycles

Hero's core business remains the commuter motorcycle franchise.

Strengths:

- strong brand trust
- high rural/semi-urban relevance
- wide service network
- replacement demand
- scale economics

Risks:

- moderate structural growth
- high competition from Honda, TVS, and Bajaj
- price sensitivity in entry-level customers

DCF treatment:

- Core motorcycles support stable FCFF.
- They do not justify aggressive terminal growth alone.

### 6.2 Premium motorcycles

Hero is trying to expand beyond its commuter image with premium products and partnerships.

Positive factors:

- higher realization per unit
- potential margin expansion
- brand repositioning
- Xtreme, Glamour, Harley-Davidson X440, Mavrick and other launches

Risks:

- brand perception challenge
- strong competition from Royal Enfield, Bajaj, TVS, Yamaha, and Honda
- premium execution still needs longer evidence

DCF treatment:

- Base case includes moderate success.
- Bull case includes stronger premium contribution.

### 6.3 Scooters

Scooters are an important opportunity because Hero has historically been stronger in motorcycles than scooters.

Positive factors:

- March 2026 scooter dispatch growth was strong.
- Models such as Xoom and Destini support portfolio expansion.
- Urban and young-user demand can support growth.

Risks:

- Honda Activa dominance
- TVS and Suzuki strength
- electric scooter disruption

DCF treatment:

- Scooter growth supports base case but requires sustained share gain to justify bull case.

### 6.4 EV / VIDA

VIDA is one of Hero's most important long-term strategic options.

Positive factors:

- India electric two-wheeler market is growing.
- VIDA registrations surged in FY26.
- Hero has strategic exposure through VIDA, Ather, Zero Motorcycles, and Euler Motors.

Risks:

- TVS, Bajaj, Ather and Ola are aggressive competitors.
- EV margins may initially be weaker than ICE margins.
- battery cost, charging ecosystem, and product software matter.
- EV market share is not guaranteed.

DCF treatment:

- EV is not treated as guaranteed base-case high-margin growth.
- EV supports bull-case upside but also appears as bear-case disruption risk.

### 6.5 Exports

Exports provide a path to diversify away from Indian rural cycles.

Positive factors:

- FY25 exports rose strongly.
- FY26 global business dispatches improved.
- Global footprint spans many emerging markets.

Risks:

- currency and macro volatility
- country-specific demand and regulation
- execution and distribution risk

DCF treatment:

- Exports support medium-term growth assumptions.
- Still not enough to justify high terminal growth without sustained execution.

## 7. Industry validation

The Indian two-wheeler industry is supported by:

- rural recovery
- improving replacement cycle
- premiumisation
- GST/tax benefits on smaller motorcycles
- electric two-wheeler adoption
- exports
- product refreshes

The industry is also exposed to:

- commodity/input cost inflation
- interest rates and consumer financing
- rural income volatility
- competitive pricing
- EV transition risk
- regulatory and subsidy changes

For Hero, the key issue is whether it can grow faster than the base industry through product mix, scooter recovery, export growth, and VIDA.

## 8. Peer comparison framework

Hero should be compared with Bajaj Auto, TVS Motor, and Eicher Motors.

### Peer context

| Company | Business positioning | Valuation implication |
|---|---|---|
| Hero MotoCorp | Commuter motorcycle leader, improving EV/scooter/premium exposure | Mature quality, lower growth than premium peers |
| Bajaj Auto | Premium motorcycles, exports, three-wheelers, strong margins | Higher growth/export valuation premium |
| TVS Motor | Strong scooter, EV, premium, racing/premium image | Higher growth and higher valuation |
| Eicher Motors | Royal Enfield premium franchise | Premium brand and higher valuation multiple |

### Relative valuation interpretation

Current public peer data suggests:

- Hero trades around 19x-21x earnings depending on price/date source.
- Bajaj Auto trades at a higher PE around the high-20s to low-30s.
- TVS trades at much higher multiples.
- Eicher trades at a higher PE due to premium brand economics.

Interpretation:

Hero is cheaper than some peers because it has lower historical sales growth and stronger commuter exposure. This discount is reasonable unless Hero proves durable acceleration in premium, scooter, EV, and exports.

## 9. WACC build-up

### Cost of equity logic

Base cost of equity is estimated using a practical CAPM-style framework:

Cost of equity = risk-free rate + beta × equity risk premium + company-specific risk premium

For the final v1 model:

| Scenario | WACC | Reason |
|---|---:|---|
| Bear | 14.0% | Higher transition risk, weaker growth, margin pressure |
| Base | 12.0% | Mature Indian equity with strong balance sheet but EV/competition risk |
| Bull | 10.75% | Better growth visibility and market confidence |

Hero has low debt, so WACC is dominated by cost of equity rather than cost of debt. A lower WACC is not automatically justified because the company faces sector transition risk.

## 11. Sensitivity interpretation

The valuation is most sensitive to:

1. WACC
2. Terminal growth
3. Whether FY26 momentum continues into FY27-FY30
4. EV and premium segment execution
5. Whether operating margin stays near 14%-15% or improves sustainably

A small reduction in WACC or a small increase in terminal growth can materially raise value because terminal value is a large part of the DCF. Therefore, the final decision should not rely on the bull case alone.

## 12. Literature-based review

### Damodaran lens

- Value must be linked to cash flows, growth, and risk.
- FCFF should be discounted at WACC.
- Valuation is uncertain and must be updated with new information.
- Hero is easier to value than young/high-loss companies, but EV transition makes future assumptions uncertain.

### Fisher lens

- Hero's distribution, brand, and product pipeline are strengths.
- Management execution in premium, scooters, EV, and exports must be monitored.
- Long-term growth runway is not guaranteed because the core commuter market is mature.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- High ROCE and quality matter, but entry valuation matters equally.
- P/E re-rating will depend on whether Hero proves sustained growth acceleration.
- Mature companies can disappoint if bought without sufficient margin of safety.

### Malkiel lens

- Do not overestimate the ability to forecast one company precisely.
- Compare against passive benchmarks.
- Avoid overconfidence in one-point DCF values.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- Hero may reduce portfolio risk because it is large, profitable, and low leverage.
- However, position size should depend on valuation, expected return, and correlation with other auto holdings.

### Stochastic DCF lens

- Scenario valuation is more appropriate than a single point estimate.
- EV transition and premiumisation are uncertain, so bull-case assumptions must not become base-case assumptions too early.

## 14. Final conclusion v1

Hero MotoCorp is a fundamentally strong company with improving operating momentum and attractive optionality from premium motorcycles, scooters, exports, and EVs. Its balance sheet and cash-flow quality are strong, and the company deserves to remain in the research universe.

However, under the base-case DCF, the stock does not offer the required 25% margin of safety at the current price proxy. The valuation becomes interesting only if the price corrects meaningfully or if Hero demonstrates that FY26 growth momentum can be sustained into a multi-year growth cycle with stable or improving margins.

### Final view

> Watchlist / Quality Business — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Update after FY26 audited results and re-run DCF.
