## 1. Final investment-style conclusion

NALCO is a financially strong, integrated Indian aluminium and alumina producer with low leverage, strong FY25 and 9M FY26 profitability, high ROCE, dividend support, captive resource advantages, and operational improvement. However, it is a cyclical commodity company. Its earnings and cash flows are strongly influenced by alumina prices, LME aluminium prices, input costs, energy/coal availability, currency, global supply disruptions, and commodity-cycle sentiment.

Therefore, NALCO should not be valued using a simple peak-earnings DCF. The correct approach is a normalized cyclical DCF, supported by sensitivity analysis and a higher margin-of-safety requirement than a stable consumer or pharma company.

Based on final v1 assumptions and current market price proxy of approximately Rs 417 per share, NALCO does not qualify as a base-case margin-of-safety candidate. Even the bull-case value is close to, but still below or around, the current market price proxy depending on commodity assumptions.

### Final classification v1

> Watchlist / Cyclical Quality PSU — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Attractive only if purchased closer to normalized-cycle value or if the analyst has strong conviction that elevated alumina/aluminium spreads can remain structurally higher.

### Final valuation output v1

| Scenario | Intrinsic value per share | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Bear | ~Rs 118 | Downside if commodity prices normalize lower and WACC rises |
| Base | ~Rs 237 | Normalized-cycle value; well below current price |
| Bull | ~Rs 412 | Strong commodity and operating-efficiency case, close to current price |

Current price proxy used: Rs 417 per share.

Base-case 35% MOS buy-zone price: ~Rs 154 per share.

Because NALCO is cyclical, the required margin of safety is set at 35%, not 25%.

## 3. Business overview

NALCO is an integrated bauxite-alumina-aluminium-power complex. It is one of India's important aluminium producers and a major alumina producer/exporter. Its integrated model gives it cost advantages compared with less integrated aluminium producers.

Key business segments:

1. Bauxite mining
2. Alumina refining
3. Aluminium smelting
4. Captive power
5. Chemicals / alumina business
6. Aluminium metal sales

Key advantages:

- integrated operations
- captive bauxite
- alumina cost advantage
- PSU backing
- low leverage
- dividend-paying profile

Key limitation:

- commodity-cycle dependence

## 7. Commodity and industry validation

Aluminium and alumina markets are affected by:

- LME aluminium prices
- alumina prices
- energy and coal costs
- China aluminium production
- global smelter disruptions
- geopolitical supply disruptions
- infrastructure and automobile demand
- currency movements
- import/export policies

Recent global aluminium news has shown supply disruptions and rising aluminium prices, including issues in the Gulf region and Strait of Hormuz disruption. Such events can benefit producers like NALCO in the short term through stronger realizations, but they can reverse when supply normalizes.

DCF implication:

- Peak-cycle earnings should not be extrapolated.
- Base case should use normalized FCFF.
- Bull case can reflect supportive aluminium/alumina prices and operating efficiency.
- Bear case should reflect commodity price normalization and margin compression.

## 10. WACC build-up

### Base WACC: 12.5%

Reasoning:

- NALCO has low leverage and PSU backing, which reduce financial distress risk.
- However, commodity-cycle earnings are volatile.
- Market risk and commodity risk justify a higher required return than for stable consumer companies.
- A company-specific risk premium is needed because earnings depend heavily on aluminium and alumina prices.

### Bear WACC: 14.5%

Used when:

- commodity prices normalize lower,
- margins compress,
- China/global supply pressure increases,
- capex returns are uncertain,
- investors demand higher commodity risk premium.

### Bull WACC: 11.5%

Used when:

- prices remain supportive,
- operational efficiency sustains,
- balance sheet remains very strong,
- market confidence in cash flows improves.

## 12. Sensitivity interpretation

NALCO's valuation is most sensitive to:

1. normalized FCFF
2. alumina prices
3. aluminium LME prices
4. captive power and coal costs
5. WACC / commodity risk premium
6. terminal growth
7. capex and expansion project returns
8. dividend payout versus reinvestment

For cyclical stocks, the biggest risk is using peak-cycle earnings as normalized earnings. A strict margin-of-safety requirement is therefore necessary.

## 13. Literature-based review

### Damodaran lens

- Cyclical companies should be valued using normalized earnings and cash flows.
- DCF must link value to sustainable cash flow, not temporary commodity prices.
- Relative valuation can be misleading because low PE may reflect peak earnings.

### Fisher lens

- NALCO is not a classic Fisher-style growth compounder.
- The key qualitative strength is cost position and integrated resource advantage rather than brand or customer moat.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- PSU and commodity stocks can deliver strong returns when cycle and valuation align.
- However, they are vulnerable to P/E contraction and earnings normalization.
- Entry price matters more than recent profit growth.

### Malkiel lens

- Avoid overconfidence in commodity price forecasts.
- Compare active stock selection with broad benchmark risk.
- Do not extrapolate recent price momentum into long-term value.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- NALCO can diversify a portfolio away from consumer/auto/pharma but adds commodity beta.
- Position size should be controlled because drawdowns can be sharp.

### Stochastic DCF lens

- Scenario analysis is essential because cash flows depend on uncertain commodity prices.
- A future enhancement could use Monte Carlo simulation for aluminium/alumina prices.

## 15. Final conclusion v1

NALCO is a strong cyclical PSU with an integrated cost advantage, low debt, strong recent profitability and supportive dividend characteristics. FY25 and 9M FY26 performance were excellent, supported by operational efficiency and favourable commodity conditions.

However, the current market price appears to already reflect a strong commodity cycle. Under normalized-cycle DCF assumptions, the base-case intrinsic value is materially below current price. The stock becomes attractive only if purchased at a much lower price or if the analyst has high conviction that elevated commodity spreads are structurally sustainable.

### Final view

> Watchlist / Cyclical Quality PSU — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Revisit after FY26 audited results and updated aluminium/alumina cycle assumptions.
