## 1. Executive conclusion

Hero MotoCorp is a high-quality, mature Indian two-wheeler franchise with strong brand recall, large distribution reach, a healthy balance sheet, shareholder-return discipline, and improving momentum from rural recovery, premium motorcycles, scooters, exports, and EV initiatives under VIDA.

However, under the current first-pass base-case DCF assumptions, the stock does not yet meet a 25% margin-of-safety requirement. The business quality is good, but valuation depends on whether Hero can sustainably accelerate growth beyond its historically modest 5-year sales growth through premiumisation, scooters, EVs, exports, and product refreshes.

Current research classification:

> Deep Research in Progress — First-pass DCF completed, but final investment-style conclusion requires annual-report reconciliation, peer comparison, industry outlook validation, and final WACC/FCFF normalization.

## 2. Business overview

Hero MotoCorp is one of India's largest two-wheeler manufacturers and remains a dominant player in commuter motorcycles. Its core strength is built on:

1. Mass-market motorcycle leadership
2. Rural and semi-urban distribution
3. Strong brand recall
4. Large dealer and service network
5. High replacement and entry-level demand exposure
6. Increasing push into premium motorcycles, scooters, EVs, and international markets

Key strategic areas for the current valuation cycle:

- core 100cc-125cc motorcycle recovery
- premium motorcycle launches
- scooter market share improvement
- EV expansion through VIDA
- export market growth
- parts and accessories revenue
- margin expansion from pricing, mix, and operating leverage

## 4. Industry and macro context

The Indian two-wheeler industry is recovering after a prolonged weak phase, supported by:

- rural demand improvement
- better marriage-season demand
- easing inflation
- tax benefits and lower interest-rate expectations
- premium motorcycle traction
- scooter demand recovery
- EV adoption
- export growth

Industry reports and market news suggest FY26 momentum remained healthy, with rural recovery and premiumisation as key demand drivers. However, FY27 could face softer growth if macro conditions weaken, if commodity costs rise, or if competition increases.

DCF implication:

- Base case should assume mid-single-digit to high-single-digit growth, not aggressive double-digit growth forever.
- Bull case can assume stronger growth if Hero gains share in scooters, premium motorcycles, EVs, and exports.
- Bear case should reflect possible slowdown in rural demand, EV disruption, and competitive pressure.

## 5. Growth-driver review

### 5.1 Core commuter motorcycle franchise

Hero's core franchise remains the mass-market motorcycle segment. This supports volume stability and provides the cash-flow foundation for the DCF.

Positive factors:

- large installed customer base
- trusted brand
- rural/semi-urban penetration
- replacement demand
- distribution strength

Risks:

- lower structural growth than premium/scooter segments
- pressure from Honda and other competitors
- limited pricing power in entry-level categories

DCF implication:

The core business supports stable FCFF but does not justify aggressive terminal growth on its own.

### 5.2 Premium motorcycles

Hero has been trying to improve its premium positioning through models such as Xtreme and Harley-Davidson partnership products. Premiumisation can improve realization per unit and margins.

Positive factors:

- higher realization
- margin expansion potential
- brand repositioning

Risks:

- Hero's historical strength is commuter motorcycles, not premium motorcycles
- strong competition from Bajaj, TVS, Royal Enfield, Yamaha, and Honda
- execution risk in brand perception

DCF implication:

Premium growth supports bull-case margin expansion but should not dominate the base case until sustained market-share evidence improves.

### 5.3 Scooters

Scooters remain an opportunity area because Hero historically has not been as dominant in scooters as in motorcycles.

Positive factors:

- urban demand
- female/young rider segment
- potential cross-sell through dealer network

Risks:

- Honda Activa dominance
- TVS and Suzuki strength
- EV scooters changing the competitive structure

DCF implication:

Scooter share improvement is an important optionality driver.

### 5.4 EV / VIDA transition

Hero's VIDA EV business creates long-term optionality but also uncertainty. EV adoption may disrupt ICE economics, dealer economics, product positioning, and capital allocation.

Positive factors:

- EV market growth potential
- Hero has scale and distribution
- affordable EV launches can expand addressable market

Risks:

- Ola Electric, Ather, TVS, Bajaj, and others have strong EV presence
- EV economics may pressure margins initially
- battery costs, technology, software, and charging ecosystem matter
- market share in EV is not guaranteed

DCF implication:

EV should be treated as a strategic option in the bull case and as a disruption risk in the bear case. It should not be valued as guaranteed high-margin growth until execution evidence improves.

### 5.5 Exports

Export growth can diversify Hero away from the domestic cycle. Recent quarterly commentary and market reports highlight exports as a support factor.

Positive factors:

- geographic diversification
- higher growth markets
- reduced dependence on Indian rural cycle

Risks:

- currency volatility
- local competition
- regulatory and distribution challenges in overseas markets

DCF implication:

Exports support long-term growth but require execution and market-specific validation.

## 6. Historical financial interpretation

Hero has strong profitability and cash generation, but historical growth has been moderate. Public financial summaries show FY25 sales around Rs 40,923 crore and TTM sales around Rs 44,403 crore in earlier first-pass analysis. The company has strong ROCE and low debt, supporting lower financial risk.

Key interpretation:

- High ROCE supports quality classification.
- Low debt supports lower bankruptcy risk.
- Dividend yield and shareholder returns are positive.
- Modest 5-year sales growth limits aggressive DCF assumptions.
- FCFF should be normalized around operating cash flow less capex, not simply PAT.

## 8. WACC reasoning

### Base WACC: 12.0%

Reasoning:

- India equity risk requires a double-digit cost of equity.
- Hero has low financial leverage, reducing financial risk.
- Business is mature and profitable, but faces EV and competitive transition risk.
- 12% is suitable for a mature but transition-exposed Indian equity.

### Bear WACC: 14.0%

Used when:

- EV disruption increases,
- competitive intensity rises,
- rural demand weakens,
- margins compress,
- market requires a higher risk premium.

### Bull WACC: 10.5%

Used when:

- market confidence improves,
- growth visibility improves,
- Hero demonstrates strong execution across premium, EV, scooter, and export categories.

## 11. Literature-based quality checklist

### Damodaran lens

- DCF must link value to cash flows, growth, and risk.
- FCFF should be discounted at WACC.
- Valuation is not precise; scenario range is necessary.
- New information should update valuation.

### Fisher lens

- Need to evaluate whether Hero has enough long-term growth runway.
- Check management execution in premium, scooter, EV, and export categories.
- Brand, distribution, and product pipeline matter.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- ROCE and growth both matter.
- P/E expansion or contraction can dominate returns.
- Mature quality companies need reasonable entry price.

### Malkiel / risk-control lens

- Compare active stock selection against benchmarks.
- Avoid overconfidence in point forecasts.
- Diversification and valuation discipline matter.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- Portfolio fit should consider drawdown, correlation, and position size.
- Single-company quality does not automatically make it a good portfolio addition.
