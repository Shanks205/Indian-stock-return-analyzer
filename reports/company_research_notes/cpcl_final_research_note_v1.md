## 1. Final investment-style conclusion

CPCL is a cyclical refining company that has delivered a major FY26 turnaround, supported by strong gross refining margins, high capacity utilization, improved crude throughput, better operating efficiency and strong Q3/Q4 FY26 profitability. FY26 profit surged sharply compared with weak FY25 profitability.

However, CPCL should not be valued using peak quarterly earnings or a simple low-PE approach. Refining companies are extremely sensitive to gross refining margin (GRM), crude/product spreads, inventory gains/losses, crude sourcing, forex, working capital, regulatory pricing and the global refining cycle.

Based on final v1 normalized-cycle DCF assumptions and a current price proxy of approximately Rs 1,094 per share, CPCL does not qualify as a base-case margin-of-safety candidate under a 40% required MOS. The base-case valuation is below current price, while the bull case becomes attractive only if elevated GRMs remain sustainable and operating efficiency continues.

### Final classification v1

> Watchlist / Deep Cyclical Refining Candidate — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Attractive only if purchased with a large margin of safety or if the analyst has high conviction that current GRM strength is structurally sustainable rather than cyclical.

## 3. Business overview

CPCL is a refinery and petro-products company and an Indian Oil Corporation group company. Its key business is refining crude oil into petroleum products. CPCL's performance depends on the spread between product prices and crude oil costs, usually captured through gross refining margin.

Key drivers:

1. Crude throughput
2. Capacity utilization
3. Gross refining margin
4. Crude sourcing flexibility
5. Product slate and distillate yield
6. Inventory gains/losses
7. Forex movement
8. Working-capital funding
9. Maintenance shutdowns
10. Regulatory and taxation environment

Key strengths:

- IOC parentage
- refining infrastructure
- high FY26 capacity utilization
- strong FY26 GRM recovery
- dividend support during good cycle

Key limitation:

- earnings are highly cyclical and GRM-sensitive

## 6. Industry validation

Refining is a spread business. Refiners do not simply benefit from high crude prices; they benefit when the value of refined products exceeds crude/feedstock cost plus operating cost.

Important refining variables:

- Brent crude price
- diesel/gasoline/ATF cracks
- LPG/naphtha/fuel oil spreads
- Russian/discounted crude availability or lack thereof
- global refinery outages
- demand from aviation, transport and industry
- shipping and freight costs
- exchange rate
- inventory gains/losses
- refinery utilization and planned shutdowns

In FY26, CPCL benefited from strong throughput, improved operating efficiency, better distillate yields and supportive product cracks. However, refining margins are globally cyclical and often mean revert.

DCF implication:

- Bear case should assume GRM normalization.
- Base case should use normalized mid-cycle FCFF.
- Bull case can reflect sustained strong GRMs and high utilization.
- High MOS is essential.

## 7. Growth-driver analysis

### 7.1 Gross refining margin recovery

GRM is the most important value driver.

Positive factors:

- FY26 GRMs improved materially.
- Q3 and Q4 margins were strong.
- Better distillate yield and cost control helped earnings.

Risks:

- GRMs are cyclical.
- Global refinery capacity additions can pressure margins.
- Product crack spreads can collapse.
- Inventory losses can offset operating profit.

DCF treatment:

- GRM strength supports bull case.
- Base case uses normalized FCFF and does not extrapolate FY26 peak profitability.

### 7.2 Throughput and capacity utilization

CPCL reported strong throughput and capacity utilization.

Positive factors:

- stronger product yield

Risks:

- unplanned outages

DCF treatment:

### 7.3 IOC parentage

CPCL benefits from Indian Oil group association.

DCF treatment:

### 7.4 Dividend support

Positive factors:

Risks:

- high dividends can reduce reinvestment flexibility

DCF treatment:

- Dividend is a return component, not a substitute for intrinsic value.

## 8. Cash-flow and working-capital considerations

Refining companies can show large swings in working capital because crude and product inventories are large. Reported PAT can differ materially from free cash flow due to:

- inventory valuation gains/losses
- crude purchases and payable timing
- receivable cycles
- tax timing
- refinery maintenance capex
- expansion capex
- borrowings for working capital

Open questions before final audited update:

1. What is FY26 CFO?
2. How much of profit converted into cash?
3. How much working-capital release or absorption occurred?
4. What is maintenance capex versus growth capex?
5. What is net debt after dividends?
6. Were there significant inventory gains?

DCF treatment:

The final v1 model uses normalized FCFF instead of reported PAT because FY26 profit is cycle-driven.

## 13. Literature-based review

### Damodaran lens

- Cyclical firms should be valued on normalized earnings and cash flows.
- Low PE during a strong cycle can be misleading.
- DCF assumptions must reflect sustainable mid-cycle cash flow, not peak GRM.

### Fisher lens

- CPCL is not a classic Fisher growth compounder.
- Quality depends more on operating efficiency, refinery complexity and parent support than on brand or consumer moat.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- Cyclicals can deliver large returns when bought at depressed earnings and low expectations.
- Buying after a profit surge can be risky if the cycle is already priced in.
- Entry price is critical.

### Malkiel lens

- Avoid overconfidence in forecasting crude/product spreads.
- Compare active cyclical bets against broad-market alternatives.
- Do not extrapolate recent share-price or earnings momentum.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- CPCL adds energy/refining exposure but increases cyclical and commodity beta.
- Position size should be small unless there is a very large valuation gap.

### Stochastic DCF lens

- Scenario valuation is essential.
- A future Monte Carlo model using GRM and crude spread assumptions would improve the project.

## 15. Final conclusion v1

CPCL has delivered an excellent FY26 turnaround driven by higher throughput, improved operational efficiency and much stronger refining margins. Q3 and Q4 FY26 were especially strong, and the company rewarded shareholders with dividends.

However, CPCL remains a deep cyclical refining company. The base-case DCF does not provide the required 40% margin of safety at the current price proxy. The bull case is attractive, but it depends on sustained strong refining margins that may not be conservative enough for a base-case investment decision.

### Final view
