## 1. Final investment-style conclusion

Fiem Industries is a fundamentally good Indian auto-ancillary business with strong exposure to the two-wheeler industry, automotive lighting, signalling equipment, rear-view mirrors, and emerging electronic/EV-related components. The company has delivered strong FY25 and 9M FY26 performance, with healthy revenue growth, margin improvement, low finance cost, strong OEM relationships, and improving cash flow.

However, at the current market price proxy of approximately Rs 2,236 per share, the stock does not qualify as a base-case margin-of-safety candidate under a 25% MOS requirement. The business quality is attractive, but the market price appears to already discount a meaningful amount of future growth.

### Final classification v1

> Quality growth business / Watchlist candidate — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. A better entry would require either a meaningful price correction or much stronger evidence that 14%+ EBITDA margins and double-digit revenue growth are sustainable over a multi-year period.

### Final valuation output v1

| Scenario | Intrinsic value per share | Interpretation |
|---|---:|---|
| Bear | ~Rs 562 | Severe downside if growth/margins normalize and WACC rises |
| Base | ~Rs 1,092 | Below current market price; no 25% MOS |
| Bull | ~Rs 1,774 | Upside case still below current price proxy |

Current price proxy used: Rs 2,236 per share.

Base-case 25% MOS buy-zone price: ~Rs 819 per share.

## 3. Business overview

Fiem Industries is a leading manufacturer of automotive lighting, signalling equipment and rear-view mirrors in India. Its business is closely linked to the Indian two-wheeler industry and the production cycles of major OEM customers.

Key product areas include:

1. Head lamps
2. Tail lamps
3. Blinkers and signalling equipment
4. Rear-view mirrors
5. Plastic moulded parts
6. LED lighting solutions
7. Automotive electronics and EV-related lighting/components

Key customer categories include two-wheeler OEMs and selected four-wheeler/commercial vehicle customers. Public investor material highlights major clients such as TVS, Honda, Yamaha and Suzuki.

| PAT | Rs 165.8 cr | Rs 205.0 cr | 23.6% |
| Net profit margin | 8.2% | 8.5% | Improvement |

Interpretation:

- Revenue growth was strong and above the broader mature-auto growth rate.
- Profit growth was higher than revenue growth, showing operating leverage.
- Finance cost declined sharply, supporting profit growth.
- Operating margin remained around 13.3%, before improving further in Q3 FY26.
- Fiem is not a highly leveraged business, which improves financial quality.

## 5. Cash-flow reconciliation

FY25 public cash-flow data indicates:

| Item | FY24 | FY25 | YoY change |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| CFO | Rs 134.9 cr | Rs 233.1 cr | 72.8% |
| CFI | Rs -68.6 cr | Rs -85.7 cr | Higher investment outflow |
| CFF | Rs -43.1 cr | Rs -55.2 cr | Higher financing outflow |
| Net cash flow | Rs 23.0 cr | Rs 92.3 cr | Strong improvement |

Interpretation:

- FY25 CFO improved strongly and exceeded PAT, which is a positive cash-conversion signal.
- Free-cash-flow proxy after investment outflow is materially below PAT, so DCF should not rely only on accounting earnings.
- The base DCF uses a normalized current FCFF proxy of Rs 185 crore, below FY25 CFO but above simple FCF after all investing outflows, to reflect operating strength and FY26 momentum.
- The final model is intentionally conservative because auto-ancillary companies require continuous reinvestment, tooling, working capital, and customer-linked capex.

## 7. Industry validation

Fiem's growth is closely tied to the Indian automobile cycle, especially two-wheelers. FY25 and FY26 data indicate a strong recovery in two-wheeler production and demand, supported by rural recovery, exports, premiumisation and EV adoption.

Positive industry factors:

- two-wheeler production recovery
- rural demand improvement
- export recovery
- premiumisation
- LED content increase per vehicle
- EV two-wheeler growth
- higher electronic content in vehicles

Risks:

- cyclical auto demand
- OEM production volatility
- raw material cost changes
- customer pricing pressure
- model-mix changes
- EV transition changing component requirements
- customer concentration risk

DCF implication:

- Base case can assume double-digit near-term growth fading over time.
- Bear case should assume revenue growth and margins normalize downward.
- Bull case requires sustained OEM growth, content-per-vehicle improvement and durable 14%+ margins.

## 8. Growth-driver analysis

### 8.1 Automotive lighting leadership

Fiem's core competence is automotive lighting and signalling equipment. LED lighting migration can increase content per vehicle and support growth above vehicle-volume growth.

Positive factors:

- LED adoption
- premium vehicle content increase
- replacement of older lighting systems
- design and tooling relationships with OEMs

Risks:

- pricing pressure from OEMs
- technology changes
- competition from other component suppliers

DCF treatment:

- Supports base-case growth.
- Does not alone justify very high terminal growth.

### 8.2 Two-wheeler OEM relationships

Fiem has strong exposure to major two-wheeler OEMs. This is a strength because OEM relationships are difficult to build and require quality, delivery and engineering capability.

Positive factors:

- customer stickiness
- recurring model-level supply
- operating leverage as OEM volumes grow

Risks:

- customer concentration
- bargaining power of OEMs
- loss of platform / model supply can affect revenue

DCF treatment:

- Customer relationships support growth visibility.
- Customer concentration justifies a company-specific risk premium in WACC.

### 8.3 EV and electronics opportunity

EV two-wheelers require lighting, signalling, electronic and plastic components. Fiem can benefit if EV adoption increases and if it wins platform-level supply with EV OEMs.

Positive factors:

- EV two-wheeler growth
- LED/electronic content
- potential new OEM relationships

Risks:

- EV OEM market share shifts quickly
- technology and supplier preferences may change
- pricing can become aggressive
- EV component opportunity may not offset any ICE platform weakness automatically

DCF treatment:

- EV supports bull case.
- Base case includes moderate EV benefit but avoids assuming guaranteed high-margin EV growth.

### 8.4 Margin expansion

Q3 FY26 margins improved to around 14.2%-14.25%, helped by operating leverage, efficiency, product mix and customer escalation benefits.

Positive factors:

- operating leverage
- product mix
- efficiency improvement
- customer escalation benefits

Risks:

- margin improvement may not be permanent
- raw material inflation
- OEM price renegotiation
- mix reversal

DCF treatment:

- Base case does not assume indefinite margin expansion.
- Bull case assumes more durable 14%+ operating performance.

## 10. WACC build-up

### Base WACC: 13.25%

Reasoning:

- India equity risk requires a double-digit cost of equity.
- Fiem is a midcap auto ancillary, so it deserves a higher risk premium than a large mature company like Hero MotoCorp.
- Customer concentration, auto-cycle risk and liquidity risk justify a company-specific premium.
- Low financial leverage reduces financial distress risk, but business cyclicality remains.

### Bear WACC: 15.5%

Used when:

- two-wheeler demand weakens,
- margins normalize below current levels,
- OEM pricing pressure increases,
- EV/customer concentration risk rises.

### Bull WACC: 11.75%

Used when:

- growth visibility improves,
- margin durability is proven,
- EV/electronics platform wins improve confidence,
- cash-flow conversion remains strong.

## 12. Sensitivity interpretation

Fiem's DCF is highly sensitive to:

1. normalized FCFF level
2. terminal value assumptions
3. sustainable EBITDA margin
4. long-term revenue growth
5. WACC / company-specific risk premium
6. customer concentration and OEM production cycle

Because the company is smaller than Hero MotoCorp and more exposed to OEM platform cycles, the base-case WACC is higher and the margin-of-safety requirement should remain strict.

## 13. Literature-based review

### Damodaran lens

- FCFF should be discounted at WACC.
- Valuation must be linked to cash flows, not only revenue growth or stock price momentum.
- Fiem's strong growth story must be translated into normalized FCFF.
- Relative valuation cannot justify value if the whole auto-ancillary sector is expensive.

### Fisher lens

- Fiem has attractive customer relationships and product specialization.
- The key qualitative question is whether its OEM relationships, R&D and product capabilities create durable growth.
- Customer checks and scuttlebutt would be useful before assigning high-confidence bull assumptions.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- Growth plus ROCE can create wealth, but entry valuation matters.
- P/E expansion may have already occurred if the market is pricing in EV and margin improvement.
- Midcap quality names can de-rate sharply when growth disappoints.

### Malkiel lens

- Avoid overconfidence in a stock that has already performed well.
- Compare the active stock idea against passive benchmark alternatives.
- Do not extrapolate recent strong quarters too far.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- Auto ancillary exposure may correlate with Hero MotoCorp and broader two-wheeler demand.
- Portfolio construction should avoid overconcentration in one auto-cycle theme.

### Stochastic DCF lens

- Scenario valuation is more appropriate than a single point estimate.
- EV and margin improvement should be treated as uncertain future states, not guaranteed outcomes.

## 15. Final conclusion v1

Fiem Industries is a strong and improving auto-ancillary business with attractive exposure to automotive lighting, LED migration, two-wheeler recovery, EV components, and long-standing OEM relationships. The company's FY25 and Q3 FY26 results show healthy growth and improving profitability.

However, the current market price appears to discount much of this improvement already. Under the final v1 base-case DCF, the intrinsic value is materially below the market price proxy, and even the bull case does not provide the user's required 25% margin of safety.

### Final view

> Watchlist / Quality Growth Business — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Revisit after FY26 audited annual report, full-year cash-flow statement, and evidence that 14%+ margins are sustainable.
