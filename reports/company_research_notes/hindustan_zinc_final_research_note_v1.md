## 1. Final investment-style conclusion

Hindustan Zinc is a high-quality Indian mining and metals company with strong cost leadership, integrated zinc-lead-silver operations, high EBITDA margins, strong cash generation, high dividend payouts, and significant exposure to silver prices. It is one of the strongest quality businesses among Indian commodity companies.

However, it remains a cyclical commodity business. The company's profit and cash flow are heavily influenced by zinc, lead, and silver prices, mine output, grade, cost of production, currency, royalty/mining costs, energy sourcing, and dividend/capital-allocation policy. Therefore, Hindustan Zinc should not be valued purely on peak FY26 earnings or on low headline PE during a strong metal-price cycle.

Based on final v1 assumptions and a current price proxy of approximately Rs 614 per share, Hindustan Zinc does not qualify as a base-case margin-of-safety candidate under a 35% commodity-company MOS requirement. The bull case is close to the current price, but the base-case normalized value is materially lower.

### Final classification v1

> Watchlist / High-Quality Commodity Dividend Business — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Attractive only if purchased closer to normalized-cycle value or if the analyst has strong conviction that high zinc/silver prices and cost advantages can remain structurally sustainable.

## 6. Commodity and industry validation

Hindustan Zinc is exposed to three key commodity drivers:

1. Zinc
2. Lead
3. Silver

### Zinc

Zinc demand is linked to galvanization, infrastructure, steel, automobiles, construction and industrial activity. Zinc prices can rise during strong demand or supply disruption and fall when industrial demand weakens or inventories rise.

### Silver

Silver has both industrial and investment demand. Silver strength materially helped Hindustan Zinc's recent profit mix. Q3 FY26 commentary suggested precious metal contribution to profits was very high, making silver an important valuation variable.

### Cost and production cycle

Mining companies have operating leverage. When metal prices rise and cost of production falls, profit increases sharply. The opposite also works in downturns.

DCF implication:

- Peak margins should not be treated as permanent.
- Base case should use normalized-cycle FCFF.
- Bull case can reflect sustained high metal prices and production strength.
- Bear case should reflect lower prices, higher costs and lower margins.

Silver has become a major profit contributor. Strong silver prices can lift margins and profit sharply.

Positive factors:

Risks:

- silver prices are volatile

- market can overcapitalize temporary silver strength

DCF treatment:

- Base case does not fully extrapolate peak silver margins.

Hindustan Zinc's cost leadership is one of its strongest qualities.

Risks:

DCF treatment:

### 7.4 Dividend and capital allocation

Hindustan Zinc has a strong dividend profile. It declared interim dividends, including Rs 10 per share in Q3 FY26 and Rs 11 per share with April 30, 2026 record date for FY27 first interim dividend.

Positive factors:

Risks:

- high dividends can increase leverage or reduce reinvestment flexibility
- dividend sustainability depends on commodity cycle
- balance-sheet policy matters because debt changes have been meaningful historically

DCF treatment:

- Dividends are valuable for shareholders but do not replace intrinsic valuation.
- Net debt/cash adjustment must be updated after audited FY26 statements.

## 9. WACC build-up

Reasoning:

- Hindustan Zinc is a high-quality commodity company with strong margins and cost leadership.
- However, commodity-price volatility and dividend/capital-allocation risk require a meaningful risk premium.
- Low operating cost improves resilience but does not remove cyclicality.

## 12. Literature-based review

### Damodaran lens

- Cyclical companies should be valued on normalized earnings and cash flows.
- Low PE during peak earnings can be misleading.
- DCF must connect sustainable cash flows, growth and risk.

### Fisher lens

- Hindustan Zinc is not a classic Fisher growth stock.
- Its quality lies in cost leadership, operational excellence and resource position.
- The key qualitative question is whether cost advantage and production strength remain durable.

### Basant Maheshwari / Indian market lens

- Commodity and PSU-like stocks can produce strong gains when cycle and valuation align.
- Entry price is critical.
- High dividends and high ROCE do not eliminate cycle risk.

### Malkiel lens

- Avoid overconfidence in forecasting zinc or silver prices.
- Compare active stock selection with broad-market and sector benchmarks.
- Do not extrapolate recent price momentum into long-term intrinsic value.

### Palomar / portfolio lens

- Hindustan Zinc can provide commodity and dividend exposure, but it increases cyclicality and drawdown risk.
- Position sizing must reflect commodity beta and correlation with other metals holdings.

### Stochastic DCF lens

- Scenario valuation is essential because future cash flows depend on uncertain metal prices.
- A future Monte Carlo model using zinc and silver price assumptions would be appropriate.

## 14. Final conclusion v1

Hindustan Zinc is a high-quality mining and metals business with strong margins, record FY26 production, strong profit growth and meaningful dividend support. It is superior to many commodity companies because of its integrated operations, cost position and silver exposure.

However, the current share price appears to reflect a strong commodity cycle. Under normalized-cycle DCF assumptions, the base-case intrinsic value is materially below the current price proxy. The stock becomes attractive only at a lower entry price or if the analyst has strong evidence that elevated zinc/silver prices and low cost of production can remain structurally sustainable.

### Final view

> Watchlist / High-Quality Commodity Dividend Business — Not a base-case margin-of-safety candidate at current price. Revisit after FY26 audited results and updated zinc/silver cycle assumptions.
