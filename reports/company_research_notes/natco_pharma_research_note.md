- Status: First-pass research note; needs final reconciliation before publication

## Executive summary

Natco Pharma is a low-debt Indian pharmaceutical company with strong FY25 profitability and cash generation, but its earnings profile is less stable than a classic steady compounder. The company has exposure to complex generics, oncology, international markets, and agrochemicals. This creates upside optionality, but also makes the DCF sensitive to product-launch timing, pricing pressure, and lumpy revenue recognition.

The first-pass DCF suggests that Natco is not obviously undervalued in the base case. The bull case becomes attractive if complex generics, semaglutide, and agrochemical growth sustain. The bear case is weak if FY25 profitability normalizes downward.

## Key financial observations

### FY25 annual performance

Public annual-report summaries indicate that FY25 was a record year for Natco, with high revenue, EBITDA, and profit after tax. The FY25 annual report snapshot reported total revenue of around Rs 47,840 million, EBITDA of around Rs 25,505 million, and profit after tax of around Rs 18,834 million.

Cash flow quality was strong. Public financial data indicates consolidated FY25 cash flow from operations of around Rs 16,620 million to Rs 16,968 million, depending on source presentation. Capex and intangible investment were material, so normalized free cash flow should be treated carefully.

### Q3 FY26 update

Natco reported Q3 FY26 consolidated total revenue of Rs 705.4 crore, EBITDA of Rs 216.8 crore, EBITDA margin of 30.7%, and consolidated PAT of Rs 151.3 crore.

Sequential revenue and profit volatility remain important. Q3 FY26 was significantly lower than Q2 FY26, reinforcing the need to avoid extrapolating any single quarter.

7. DCF sensitivity to normalized FCFF assumptions

## Analyst conclusion

Natco Pharma should remain in the valuation universe but requires deeper work before a final view. The company looks financially strong and has interesting growth optionality, but earnings volatility makes it unsuitable for a simplistic DCF. The correct approach is a probability-weighted or scenario-based DCF.

For the GitHub project, Natco is useful because it teaches a different valuation challenge from Hero MotoCorp and Fiem Industries:

- Hero MotoCorp: mature operating business with EV transition risk
- Fiem Industries: growth/midcap valuation sensitivity
- Natco Pharma: pharma optionality, lumpy earnings, and product-cycle risk
