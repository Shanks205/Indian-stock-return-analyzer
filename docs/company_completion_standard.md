# Company Completion Standard

This document defines the minimum standard before any company in the Indian Equity Valuation Lab can be treated as complete.

The project will not move a company to final conclusion status until all required research, reconciliation, valuation, and review steps are completed.

## Core principle

A company should not receive a final investment-style conclusion from a first-pass DCF alone.

A company is complete only after:

```text
Official filings
+ financial statement reconciliation
+ quarterly update
+ industry validation
+ peer comparison
+ WACC build-up
+ DCF model
+ scenario and sensitivity analysis
+ literature-based quality checklist
+ final margin-of-safety decision
```

## Research stages

| Stage | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Screened | Company passed initial screener or was selected for study |
| First-pass DCF | Initial valuation performed using high-level assumptions |
| Deep research in progress | Annual reports, quarterly data, industry and peer research being reviewed |
| Reconciled valuation | Historical numbers, WACC, FCFF, growth, and assumptions reconciled with sources |
| Final research note | Full analyst-style conclusion completed |
| Paper-ready | Research output is strong enough to support future SSRN-style working paper |

## Completion checklist

A company can be marked complete only when the following checklist is satisfied.

### 1. Official company filings

- Latest annual report reviewed
- At least 3 to 5 years of annual reports reviewed where available
- Latest quarterly result reviewed
- Investor presentation reviewed if available
- Earnings call transcript reviewed if available
- Management commentary extracted
- Corporate announcements checked for major events

### 2. Financial statement reconciliation

Historical table must include:

- Revenue
- EBITDA
- EBIT
- PAT
- Depreciation and amortization
- Tax expense
- Operating cash flow
- Capital expenditure
- Free cash flow
- Working capital movement
- Debt
- Cash and investments
- Shares outstanding
- ROE
- ROCE
- Margins

The model must clearly specify whether consolidated or standalone numbers are used.

### 3. Cash-flow quality review

The research note must answer:

- Are profits converting into operating cash flow?
- Is free cash flow positive and repeatable?
- Are receivables or inventory rising faster than sales?
- Is capex maintenance or growth capex?
- Are there one-off items affecting earnings or cash flow?

### 4. Business and industry validation

The research note must include:

- Industry structure
- Demand drivers
- Supply-side risks
- Regulation
- Competitive intensity
- Cyclicality
- Input-cost sensitivity
- Long-term growth outlook
- Relevant macro variables

### 5. Growth-driver analysis

The research note must explain:

- What drives revenue growth?
- What drives margin expansion or compression?
- What drives reinvestment needs?
- What can go wrong?
- Which growth drivers belong in the base case?
- Which growth drivers should remain only in the bull case?

### 6. Peer comparison

Each company should be compared with relevant peers on:

- Revenue growth
- EBITDA margin
- EBIT margin
- PAT margin
- ROE
- ROCE
- Debt-to-equity
- PE
- EV/EBITDA
- Price-to-book, where relevant
- Dividend yield, where relevant
- Free-cash-flow quality

### 7. WACC build-up

The WACC section must include:

- Risk-free rate assumption
- Equity risk premium assumption
- Beta or sector beta assumption
- Company-specific risk premium
- Cost of equity
- Cost of debt
- Tax rate
- Capital structure weights
- Bear/base/bull WACC logic

### 8. DCF model completion

DCF must include:

- FCFF bridge
- Revenue forecast
- Margin forecast
- Tax forecast
- Depreciation assumption
- Capex assumption
- Working-capital assumption
- Explicit forecast period
- Terminal value
- Enterprise value
- Net debt / net cash adjustment
- Equity value
- Intrinsic value per share

### 9. Scenario and sensitivity analysis

At minimum:

- Bear case
- Base case
- Bull case
- WACC vs terminal-growth sensitivity
- Revenue growth vs margin sensitivity
- Margin-of-safety buy-zone calculation

### 10. Literature-based quality checklist

The research note must incorporate the project literature framework:

- Damodaran valuation discipline
- Fisher business-quality and management-quality lens
- Basant Maheshwari Indian-market growth and valuation lens
- Malkiel risk, diversification, and humility lens
- Palomar portfolio risk and drawdown lens
- Stochastic DCF uncertainty/scenario lens where relevant

### 11. Final conclusion standard

The final conclusion must state:

- Business quality rating
- Financial quality rating
- Valuation attractiveness
- Margin-of-safety status
- Key upside drivers
- Key downside risks
- Confidence level
- Whether the company is:
  - Strong margin-of-safety candidate
  - Watchlist / deepen research
  - Fairly valued
  - Overvalued under base case
  - Avoid / insufficient data
