# Research Pipeline

This document describes how each company should move through the valuation workflow.

## Step 1 — Define universe

Start with Indian listed companies from a defined universe such as:

- Nifty 500
- BSE 500
- selected midcap and smallcap stocks
- custom barbell stock shortlist

## Step 2 — Apply fundamental screen

Screen companies using:

- market capitalization
- debt-to-equity
- current ratio
- ROCE
- ROE
- revenue growth
- profit growth
- operating margin
- PE vs industry PE
- promoter pledge

## Step 3 — Calculate Piotroski F-Score

Use accounting data from annual reports or financial statement exports.

The Piotroski score should be calculated, not simply copied, when possible.

## Step 4 — Build source log

For every company, maintain a source log:

| Data item | Source | Date accessed | Notes |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Annual report | Company website / exchange filing | | |
| Quarterly result | Company website / BSE / NSE | | |
| Market price | Screener / NSE / BSE | | |
| Industry report | CRISIL / ICRA / CARE / brokerage | | |
| Risk-free rate | India 10Y G-Sec source | | |
| ERP / CRP | Damodaran data | | |

## Step 5 — Historical financial analysis

Collect at least 5 years of:

- revenue
- EBITDA
- EBIT
- tax expense
- PAT
- depreciation
- capex
- operating cash flow
- free cash flow
- debt
- cash and investments
- shares outstanding

## Step 6 — Forecast assumptions

Prepare base, bear, and bull assumptions for:

- revenue growth
- EBIT margin
- tax rate
- reinvestment
- capex intensity
- working capital intensity
- WACC
- terminal growth

Every assumption should have a reason.

## Step 7 — DCF valuation

Use FCFF DCF for non-financial companies.

Avoid standard FCFF DCF for banks and financial institutions. Use bank-specific valuation methods such as P/B, ROE, residual income, and dividend discount approaches.

## Step 8 — Sensitivity analysis

Build sensitivity tables for:

- WACC vs terminal growth
- revenue growth vs EBIT margin
- margin of safety vs current price

## Step 9 — Risk and bias checks

Add the following checks:

- risk-regime check
- WACC stress test
- AI attention-bias check
- governance-risk check
- cash-conversion check

## Step 10 — Final analyst view

Each company report should end with:

- investment thesis
- key growth drivers
- key risks
- intrinsic value range
- margin of safety
- portfolio-fit score
- final view: strong candidate / watchlist / fairly valued / overvalued / avoid

## Step 11 — Research-paper conversion

For SSRN-style research, convert company-level outputs into a broader study:

- compare DCF-implied undervaluation across companies
- compare Piotroski score with valuation attractiveness
- test whether high-quality companies deserve lower margin-of-safety requirements
- compare value bucket vs growth bucket
- evaluate the barbell framework
