# Hindustan Zinc Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Hindustan Zinc Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Hindustan Zinc is a commodity-linked mining and metals company. It should not be valued by simply annualizing a strong zinc/silver price period.

Every assumption should consider:

- zinc price cycle
- silver price cycle
- lead price contribution
- mine production and grades
- energy costs
- royalty and tax burden
- USD/INR movement
- capex intensity
- dividend policy
- ownership and capital allocation
- mid-cycle margin normalization

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on recent quarterly run-rate plus normalization for commodity prices.

### Why

FY26 has benefited from strong zinc/silver conditions. Full-year results require audited reconciliation, and commodity realization can change quickly.

### Modelling rule

Use current strength as a starting point, not as a permanent cycle assumption.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate growth after a strong FY26 base.

### Why

Long-term revenue growth depends on metal prices, mining volumes, grades, silver contribution and exchange rates. Volume growth is more constrained than in asset-light sectors.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use normalized margins below peak-cycle levels.

### Why

Strong zinc and silver prices can lift margins sharply, but a mid-cycle DCF should fade margins toward sustainable levels.

### Modelling rule

Silver upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax, royalty and statutory burden

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective burden.

### Why

Mining companies face corporate tax, royalties, levies and other statutory charges. Actual burden can vary with regulation, profitability and accounting treatment.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with audited annual report and latest quarterly filing.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to mining, smelting and power asset base.

### Why

Hindustan Zinc requires mine development, smelters, processing facilities and power assets.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate-to-high capex intensity.

### Why

Capex is needed for mine development, reserve replacement, smelters, sustainability, power and safety requirements.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital normalization.

### Why

Metals businesses can have inventory, receivable and price-cycle working-capital swings.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Commodity companies should not be valued with aggressive perpetual growth assumptions. Long-term value depends more on reserves, mid-cycle margins and capital discipline.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Metal / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- commodity / ownership risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax and royalty burden
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include a commodity / ownership risk overlay because reported beta may not capture full zinc/silver-cycle and capital-allocation risk.

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |

| Revenue growth | Weak / price correction | Normalized growth | Strong price and volume support |
| Margin | Sharp normalization | Mid-cycle normalization | Elevated but not permanent |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for Hindustan Zinc is cycle normalization: current zinc/silver strength must be separated from sustainable mid-cycle earnings power.
