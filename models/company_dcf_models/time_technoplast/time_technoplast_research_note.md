## 1. Executive summary

Time Technoplast is an Indian polymer and packaging products company with exposure to industrial packaging, infrastructure products, intermediate bulk containers, composite cylinders, value-added polymer products and recycling-linked opportunities.

Time Technoplast is different from the earlier auto-component and commodity cases because its thesis is built around **value-added product mix improvement, packaging demand, composite products, operating leverage, raw-material pass-through and balance-sheet improvement**.

The public-source v1 thesis is that Time Technoplast should be modelled as a **value-added industrial packaging and polymer-products company with improving margin profile, growth in composite products, working-capital requirements and raw-material sensitivity**.

The DCF should not simply extrapolate one strong quarter. A normalized margin and value-added-product growth approach is more appropriate.

## 4. Investment thesis

Time Technoplast's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Value-added product growth | Composite and value-added products can improve margin quality. |
| Stable established business | Industrial packaging supports base revenue and scale. |
| Operating leverage | Higher volumes and mix improvement can support EBITDA growth. |
| Debt reduction | Lower debt can improve equity value and risk profile. |
| Working-capital and raw-material risk | FCF must be tested against inventory, receivables and polymer-price movements. |

The base case should use moderate revenue growth and sustainable margin improvement. The bull case can capture faster composite/value-added product adoption. The bear case should reflect raw-material pressure, working-capital stretch, margin compression and slower industrial demand.

## 6. Financial performance review

Time Technoplast reported strong Q3 FY26 and 9M FY26 results. Public result coverage reported Q3 FY26 total income of around ₹1,567.1 crore, up around 13% year-on-year. EBITDA was around ₹235.8 crore, up around 17% year-on-year, with EBITDA margin around 15.0%. PAT was around ₹126.3 crore, up around 25% year-on-year.

For 9M FY26, total income was around ₹4,432.8 crore, EBITDA around ₹655.4 crore and PAT around ₹336.9 crore. Value-added products grew faster than established products and the company continued to focus on composite and recycling segments.

The correct research treatment is:

> Time Technoplast's Q3/9M FY26 performance supports the value-added-products thesis, but the DCF must normalize raw-material, working-capital and margin assumptions before treating recent momentum as permanent.

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Industrial / Working-Capital Risk Premium
```

An industrial / working-capital risk premium is useful because reported beta may not fully capture polymer raw-material volatility, inventory risk, receivable risk, product-mix execution and capex-cycle risk.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

Time Technoplast's DCF is most sensitive to:

- revenue growth
- value-added product mix
- normalized EBITDA margin
- working-capital cycle
- raw-material pass-through
- capex intensity
- WACC
- terminal growth
