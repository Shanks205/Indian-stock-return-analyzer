# Time Technoplast Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Time Technoplast Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Time Technoplast is an industrial packaging and value-added polymer-products company. It should not be valued by simply extrapolating one strong quarter.

Every assumption should consider:

- industrial packaging demand
- value-added product mix
- IBC and composite cylinder adoption
- raw-material price volatility
- customer pass-through ability
- working-capital cycle
- capex and capacity expansion
- debt reduction
- export and currency exposure

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus normalized Q4.

### Why

9M FY26 shows steady growth, but full-year audited data and segment mix require reconciliation.

### Modelling rule

Use current value-added-product momentum as a growth driver, but do not assume peak growth continues indefinitely.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use moderate growth that fades over time.

### Why

Industrial packaging demand, value-added products and composite products support growth, but industrial cycles and raw-material price movements can affect reported revenue.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use sustainable margins near recent improved range, but avoid aggressive permanent expansion.

### Why

Margins can improve with value-added mix, but raw-material volatility, pricing competition and utilization can pressure profitability.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary due to one-off items, incentives and accounting treatment.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to manufacturing assets, moulds, machinery and capex.

### Why

Polymer and packaging manufacturing requires plants, equipment, moulds and technical assets.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate capex intensity.

### Why

Capex is needed for IBCs, composite cylinders, value-added products, recycling/sustainability products and capacity expansion.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital assumptions.

### Why

Inventory and receivables can materially affect free cash flow, especially when raw-material prices move.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Value-added products support growth, but industrial cyclicality and working-capital requirements prevent aggressive perpetual assumptions.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty / packaging or industrial peers
- India equity risk premium
- industrial / working-capital risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include an industrial and working-capital risk overlay because reported beta may not capture raw-material, inventory and product-mix execution risk.
