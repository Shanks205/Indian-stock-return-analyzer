# NALCO Peer Framework v1

## Purpose

This file completes the peer-comparison layer for the NALCO public-source v1 research pack.

NALCO should not be compared mechanically with every metals company because its integrated bauxite-alumina-aluminium-power structure, PSU ownership, dividend profile and commodity exposure are different from diversified private-sector metal companies.

Peer comparison should be used as a valuation sanity check, not as a replacement for cycle-normalized DCF.

## Interpretation framework

### NALCO vs Hindalco

Hindalco is the closest broad aluminium comparison, but it has a more diversified downstream and global business profile. NALCO's integrated bauxite-alumina structure and PSU ownership create a different risk/return profile.

### NALCO vs Vedanta

Vedanta is useful for aluminium and metals-cycle comparison, but its diversified commodity exposure and leverage profile mean valuation multiples should not be applied mechanically to NALCO.

### NALCO vs Hindustan Zinc

Hindustan Zinc is useful for commodity cash generation and dividend comparison, but its commodity exposure is zinc/lead/silver rather than aluminium/alumina.

### NALCO vs NMDC

NMDC is a PSU mining reference and helps frame government ownership, dividend policy and mining-cycle exposure. It is not a direct product peer.

### NALCO vs Coal India

Coal India helps frame PSU commodity dividend expectations. It should not be used as a direct valuation anchor because the commodity, cost structure and demand drivers differ.
