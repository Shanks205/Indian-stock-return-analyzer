## 1. Executive summary

National Aluminium Company Limited, commonly known as NALCO, is a Government of India-owned Navratna CPSE under the Ministry of Mines. It is an integrated bauxite-alumina-aluminium-power company with exposure to mining, alumina refining, aluminium smelting, power generation and related operations.

NALCO is different from Hero MotoCorp and Natco Pharma because it is a commodity-cycle business. The main valuation question is not only whether the company is profitable today, but whether current margins are sustainable across the aluminium and alumina price cycle.

The public-source v1 thesis is that NALCO should be modelled as a **cyclical, integrated aluminium/alumina PSU with strong recent profitability, dividend appeal, commodity-price sensitivity, and expansion optionality**.

The DCF should not treat peak alumina or aluminium margins as permanent. A normalized-cycle approach is more appropriate.

## 2. Business overview

NALCO operates across an integrated aluminium value chain.

| Business area | Role in thesis |
|---|---|
| Bauxite mining | Input security and cost advantage |
| Alumina refining | Major profitability driver, export-linked and price-sensitive |
| Aluminium smelting | Metal production exposure to LME aluminium prices |
| Captive power | Cost support for aluminium smelting |
| Wind / solar power | Sustainability and energy-diversification support |
| Expansion projects | Long-term growth and capacity optionality |

The integrated structure can provide cost advantages, but earnings remain highly sensitive to commodity prices, energy costs, alumina spreads, aluminium prices and exchange rates.

## 3. Industry context

The aluminium industry is cyclical. Demand is linked to infrastructure, power transmission, construction, transport, packaging, renewable energy, consumer durables and industrial production.

Important industry variables for NALCO:

- LME aluminium prices
- alumina prices and spreads
- bauxite availability
- coal and power costs
- USD/INR movement
- China supply/demand dynamics
- global inventory cycle
- domestic infrastructure demand
- export realization
- environmental and carbon rules
- government ownership and dividend policy

## 4. Investment thesis

NALCO's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Integrated operations | Bauxite-alumina-aluminium-power integration supports cost competitiveness. |
| Strong recent earnings | FY25 and 9M FY26 show strong profitability, but cycle normalization is required. |
| Dividend appeal | PSU structure and strong profits support dividend relevance. |
| Commodity upside | Strong aluminium/alumina pricing can significantly boost profitability. |
| Cyclical risk | Valuation must normalize margins and avoid peak-cycle extrapolation. |

The base case should use normalized commodity-cycle assumptions. The bull case can capture stronger aluminium and alumina prices, higher volumes and expansion execution. The bear case should capture commodity-price correction, cost inflation and lower margins.

## 6. Financial performance review

NALCO reported strong FY25 performance, with operating income up materially year-on-year, net sales of around ₹16,787.6 crore and PAT around ₹5,324.7 crore on a consolidated basis. FY25 operating profit margins were very strong compared with FY24.

For Q3 FY26, NALCO reported record results. Company communication reported Q3 FY26 net profit of around ₹1,601 crore and total income of around ₹4,925 crore. For 9M FY26, revenue from operations was around ₹12,830 crore and net profit was around ₹4,098 crore.

The correct research treatment is:

> NALCO's recent profitability is strong, but DCF assumptions must reflect commodity-cycle normalization rather than assuming current margins continue forever.

| DCF input | Base-case treatment | Justification |

| Revenue base | FY26E based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4 | Current 9M performance provides a strong base, but Q4 and commodity realizations need full-year reconciliation. |
| Revenue growth | Low-to-moderate cycle-normalized growth | Aluminium demand grows, but prices are cyclical. |
| EBITDA / EBIT margin | Normalized below peak cycle | FY25/Q3 FY26 margins are strong; long-term DCF should fade toward mid-cycle profitability. |

## 8. Scenario logic

### Bear case

- Aluminium and alumina prices correct sharply
- Energy and input costs rise
- Margins revert faster than expected
- Expansion projects face delays or cost overruns
- Dividend expectations weaken
- Global demand slows

### Base case

- FY26 remains strong but margins normalize
- Volume growth remains steady
- Alumina/aluminium realization stays healthy but not peak
- Cost optimization and integration support margins
- Capex remains manageable

### Bull case

- Aluminium/alumina prices stay elevated
- Capacity expansion executes well
- Captive coal and cost optimization improve margins
- Strong dividend payouts continue
- Domestic infrastructure demand supports volume growth

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Commodity / PSU Risk Premium
```

A commodity / PSU risk premium is useful because NALCO's historical beta may not fully capture commodity-cycle risk, policy influence, capex timing and margin volatility.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

NALCO's DCF is most sensitive to:

- aluminium and alumina price assumptions
- normalized margin
- WACC
- terminal growth
- capex intensity
- dividend / capital allocation policy
