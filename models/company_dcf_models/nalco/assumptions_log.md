# NALCO Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** National Aluminium Company Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

NALCO is a cyclical commodity company. It should not be valued by simply annualizing a strong commodity-price period.

Every assumption should consider:

- aluminium price cycle
- alumina price cycle
- bauxite and input security
- energy and coal costs
- USD/INR movement
- capex intensity
- PSU ownership
- dividend policy
- commodity margin normalization

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus a normalized Q4 estimate.

### Why

9M FY26 performance is strong, but full-year results require audited reconciliation. Commodity realization can shift quickly.

### Modelling rule

Use current strength as a starting point, not as a permanent cycle assumption.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate growth after a strong FY26 base.

### Why

Long-term growth is driven by volume, capacity expansion and demand, but revenue is also heavily affected by aluminium and alumina prices.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use normalized margins below peak-cycle levels.

### Why

Strong FY25/Q3 FY26 margins may reflect favourable alumina/aluminium pricing and cost conditions. Long-term DCF should fade toward mid-cycle margins.

### Modelling rule

Commodity upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary with incentives, deferred tax, one-off items and profit mix.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with audited annual report and latest quarterly filing.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to the asset-heavy mining, refining and smelting base.

### Why

NALCO requires significant plant, mine, refinery, smelter and power assets.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate-to-high capex intensity.

### Why

Expansion projects, sustaining capex, mine development, refining, smelting, power and environmental investments require capital.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital normalization.

### Why

Commodity companies experience inventory, receivable and price-cycle working-capital swings.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Commodity companies should not be valued with aggressive perpetual growth assumptions. Long-term value depends more on cycle-normalized margins and capital discipline.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Metal / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- commodity / PSU risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include a commodity / PSU risk overlay because reported beta may not capture full commodity-cycle and policy risk.

## Scenario assumption summary

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Weak / price correction | Normalized growth | Strong price and volume support |
| Margin | Sharp normalization | Mid-cycle normalization | Elevated but not permanent |
| Capex | Higher burden | Moderate to high | Productive growth capex |
| Working capital | Less efficient | Normalized | Better cycle management |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Moderate | Slightly higher but disciplined |
| WACC | Higher | Normalized with commodity risk | Lower / stable |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for NALCO is cycle normalization: current commodity strength must be separated from sustainable mid-cycle earnings power.
