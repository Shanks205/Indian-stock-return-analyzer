## 1. Executive summary

Sharda Cropchem is an agrochemical company focused on crop-protection products, registrations, distribution, sourcing and global market access. The company follows an asset-light model compared with many manufacturing-heavy chemical businesses, relying significantly on product registrations, formulation sourcing, geographic reach and working-capital execution.

Sharda Cropchem is different from the previous commodity cases because its value is not driven by a single commodity price like aluminium, zinc or GRM. Its valuation depends on agrochemical demand, product registrations, geography mix, inventory management, receivables, gross margins, currency movement and recovery in global crop-protection markets.

The public-source v1 thesis is that Sharda Cropchem should be modelled as an **asset-light agrochemical registration-led business with strong recent recovery, global exposure, working-capital intensity and margin-cycle risk**.

The DCF should not treat one strong recovery quarter as permanent. A normalized margin and working-capital approach is more appropriate.

## 4. Investment thesis

Sharda Cropchem's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Registration-led business model | Product registrations create market access and support long-term optionality. |
| Asset-light structure | Lower manufacturing capex, but higher reliance on sourcing and working capital. |
| Strong recent recovery | Q3 FY26 showed strong revenue, EBITDA and PAT growth. |
| Global exposure | Europe and other export markets support scale but increase currency and regulatory risk. |
| Working-capital risk | Inventory and receivable cycles can materially affect free cash flow. |

The base case should use normalized agrochemical recovery assumptions. The bull case can capture stronger volume growth, better product mix and margin recovery. The bear case should capture global destocking, regulatory pressure, receivable stress and margin compression.

## 6. Financial performance review

Sharda Cropchem reported strong Q3 FY26 results. Revenue was reported at around ₹1,288.8 crore, up 39% year-on-year. EBITDA was around ₹245.5 crore, up 59% year-on-year, with EBITDA margin of 19.1%. PAT was around ₹145.1 crore, up sharply year-on-year. For 9M FY26, revenue was around ₹3,202.7 crore and PAT was around ₹362.3 crore.

Management commentary highlighted volume growth, product mix, Europe as a key contributor, stabilizing input costs and improved gross margin.

The correct research treatment is:

> Sharda Cropchem's Q3 FY26 recovery is strong, but the DCF must normalize agrochemical cycle, product mix and working-capital assumptions before treating this as sustainable long-term earnings power.

## 8. Scenario logic

### Bear case

- Agrochemical recovery slows
- Channel destocking returns
- Europe demand weakens
- Gross margins compress
- Receivables stretch and working capital absorbs cash
- Regulatory bans or registration delays affect product access
- Currency movement hurts realization

### Base case

- FY26 recovery continues but normalizes
- Product registrations support moderate growth
- Gross margins remain healthy but below extreme peaks
- Working capital improves gradually
- Capex remains manageable due to asset-light model

### Bull case

- Global agrochemical demand recovers strongly
- Europe and other export regions deliver sustained growth
- Product mix improves materially
- Input costs remain stable
- Working-capital cycle improves
- Registration pipeline converts into scalable revenue

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Agrochemical / Working-Capital Risk Premium
```

An agrochemical / working-capital risk premium is useful because observed beta may not fully capture registration risk, currency risk, inventory risk, receivable risk and global agrochemical-cycle volatility.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

Sharda Cropchem's DCF is most sensitive to:

- revenue recovery assumptions
- normalized gross and EBITDA margin
- working-capital cycle
- currency movement
- WACC
- terminal growth
