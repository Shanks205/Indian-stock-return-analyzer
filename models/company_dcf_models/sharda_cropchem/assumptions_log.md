# Sharda Cropchem Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Sharda Cropchem Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Sharda Cropchem is an agrochemical, registration-led, globally exposed and working-capital-sensitive business. It should not be valued by simply annualizing one strong recovery quarter.

Every assumption should consider:

- global agrochemical cycle
- product registrations
- Europe and other geography mix
- crop-protection demand
- channel inventory cycle
- gross margin recovery
- currency movement
- sourcing risk
- inventory and receivables
- cash conversion

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus normalized Q4.

### Why

9M FY26 and Q3 FY26 show a clear recovery, but full-year data and cash-flow quality require audited reconciliation.

### Modelling rule

Use recovery momentum as a base, but do not assume the strongest quarter continues permanently.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use moderate recovery growth that fades over time.

### Why

Product registrations, global distribution and crop-protection demand support growth, but the agrochemical industry can face destocking and regulatory cycles.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use normalized margins below the strongest recovery assumptions.

### Why

Q3 FY26 EBITDA margin was strong, but margins can be affected by product mix, geography, input costs, currency movement and competitive pricing.

### Modelling rule

Margin upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary due to geography mix, subsidiaries, one-off items and accounting treatment.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with audited annual report and latest quarterly filing.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate depreciation intensity.

### Why

The business is more asset-light than manufacturing-heavy chemical peers, although registrations and business assets still require investment.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate capex intensity.

### Why

Sharda Cropchem's asset-light model reduces manufacturing capex needs, but registrations, market development and business expansion still require capital.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital assumptions.

### Why

Inventory and receivables are central to the free cash flow story. Accounting profits can look strong while cash conversion weakens.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Product registrations support growth, but agrochemical cyclicality, regulation and working-capital risk prevent aggressive perpetual growth.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty / chemical or agrochemical peers
- India equity risk premium
- agrochemical / working-capital risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include an agrochemical and working-capital risk overlay because reported beta may not capture business-model and cash-conversion risk.
