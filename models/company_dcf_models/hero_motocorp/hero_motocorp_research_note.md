## 1. Executive summary

Hero MotoCorp is a leading Indian two-wheeler manufacturer with a strong commuter motorcycle franchise, expanding presence in scooters, premium motorcycles, exports, electric mobility through VIDA, and parts/accessories/merchandising revenue.

The company is suitable for this valuation lab because it combines:

- large listed Indian company profile
- meaningful operating history
- DCF suitability
- strong brand and distribution network
- visible growth drivers
- clear peer set
- useful interview discussion value

This is not a pure deep-value case. It is better classified as a **quality compounder / cyclical recovery / optionality valuation case**.

The base-case DCF should stay disciplined and should not assume that every optional growth driver succeeds. Premium motorcycles, EV scale-up, exports, and margin expansion should be treated as upside optionality rather than guaranteed base-case outcomes.

---

## 2. Business overview

Hero MotoCorp operates primarily in the two-wheeler industry. The core business has historically been commuter motorcycles, where affordability, mileage, brand trust, and distribution depth are critical.

The company is now broadening its growth platform across:

| Segment | Strategic importance |
|---|---|
| Commuter motorcycles | Core cash-generating business |
| Scooters | Market-share expansion opportunity |
| Premium motorcycles | Higher-margin and brand-upgrade opportunity |
| Electric vehicles | Long-term optionality through VIDA |
| Exports | Geographic diversification |
| Parts, accessories, merchandising | Higher-quality revenue and margin support |

---

## 3. Industry context

The Indian two-wheeler industry is influenced by:

- rural income trends
- replacement demand
- vehicle financing availability
- fuel prices
- commodity input costs
- regulatory and emission standards
- GST / tax treatment
- EV adoption
- competition from traditional and EV-focused players

Hero's core demand base remains connected to mass mobility and rural / semi-urban consumption. This makes the company sensitive to rural purchasing power, affordability, and macroeconomic confidence.

## 4. Investment thesis

The investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Core business strength | Hero remains a major two-wheeler franchise with brand and distribution advantages. |
| Demand recovery | Rural demand, affordability support, and replacement cycle can support near-term growth. |
| Margin profile | The company has historically demonstrated healthy operating profitability. |
| Optionality | VIDA EV, premium bikes, Harley partnership, exports, and accessories can improve long-term growth quality. |
| Valuation discipline | The DCF should avoid extrapolating a strong period permanently. |

The base case should value Hero MotoCorp as a stable, profitable two-wheeler leader. The bull case should capture stronger premiumisation, export growth, and EV scaling. The bear case should reflect demand slowdown, EV losses, competition, and margin pressure.

## 8. Bull / base / bear logic

### Bull case

The bull case can assume:

- stronger premium motorcycle execution
- faster scooter market-share gains
- better VIDA EV scaling
- higher export contribution
- stable commodity prices
- better operating leverage
- margin expansion above base case

### Base case

The base case assumes:

- core business remains stable
- revenue growth normalizes over time
- margins stay around sustainable levels
- EV and premium segments contribute gradually
- no extreme re-rating is assumed

### Bear case

The bear case should assume:

- weaker rural demand
- slower two-wheeler replacement cycle
- EV business losses continue longer
- commodity cost pressure
- stronger competition from Bajaj, TVS, Ola Electric, Ather, and others
- lower EBITDA margin and lower terminal growth

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium
```

### WACC

```text
WACC = Weight of Equity × Cost of Equity + Weight of Debt × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

### Required WACC inputs

| Input | Required source | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Risk-free rate | India 10-year government bond yield | Needs latest refresh |
| Beta | Regression beta vs Nifty 50 / Nifty Auto | Needs calculation |
| Equity risk premium | India ERP estimate | Needs source validation |
| Cost of debt | Finance cost / borrowings from annual report | Needs audited annual data |
| Tax rate | Effective tax rate from financial statements | Needs annual report reconciliation |
| Debt weight | Market value debt / total capital | Needs balance sheet refresh |
| Equity weight | Market cap / total capital | Needs market price refresh |

Hero MotoCorp's DCF is likely more sensitive to cost of equity, terminal growth, and operating margin than to cost of debt, especially if the company remains debt-light or net-cash.

```text

```
