## Assumption discipline

This assumptions log exists to avoid random DCF inputs. Every assumption should be connected to at least one of the following:

- historical financial performance
- company disclosures
- industry structure
- peer comparison
- macroeconomic context
- conservative normalization
- scenario-specific upside or downside case

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use moderate near-term growth that gradually fades over the explicit forecast period.

### Why

Hero MotoCorp is a large, mature two-wheeler company. A strong quarter or recovery cycle can support near-term growth, but long-term assumptions should normalize.

### Modelling rule

Do not permanently extrapolate a single high-growth quarter.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use margins around a sustainable historical/current range.

### Why

Margin support can come from scale, premium products, parts/accessories, and operating leverage. Margin pressure can come from EV investment, commodity costs, discounts, and competition.

### Modelling rule

Margin expansion should be limited in the base case and stronger only in the bull case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual effective tax can vary due to one-off items, incentives, and accounting adjustments.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with the latest audited annual report.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to sales, PPE, and capex patterns.

### Why

Depreciation should not be projected randomly. It should be consistent with historical asset intensity.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate capex intensity.

### Why

Hero needs continued investment in product refreshes, technology, EV, premium motorcycles, distribution, and manufacturing capability.

### Bull case

Higher capex can be productive if it supports stronger revenue growth and margin expansion.

### Bear case

Higher capex becomes a valuation drag if growth and margins disappoint.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital normalization.

### Why

Auto working capital can move with inventory cycles, dealer stocking, seasonality, and demand swings.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative long-term terminal growth.

### Why

Terminal growth must represent mature sustainable growth and should not exceed reasonable long-term assumptions.

### Modelling rule

Terminal growth should be below WACC by a safe spread.
