# CPCL Peer Framework v1

## Purpose

This file completes the peer-comparison layer for the CPCL public-source v1 research pack.

CPCL should not be compared mechanically with all oil & gas companies because it is primarily a refining business with high sensitivity to GRMs, crude/product spreads, inventory movements, throughput and working capital. Integrated marketing companies and private refining players can be useful references, but their business mix and balance-sheet structures differ.

Peer comparison should be used as a valuation sanity check, not as a replacement for cycle-normalized DCF.

## Interpretation framework

### CPCL vs Indian Oil Corporation

IOC is relevant because of parentage and refining linkage, but it also has marketing, pipelines and broader integrated operations. CPCL's earnings can be more directly sensitive to refining margins.

### CPCL vs BPCL and HPCL

BPCL and HPCL are useful PSU refining and marketing comparisons. However, marketing margins, retail networks and integration make their earnings mix different from CPCL.

### CPCL vs MRPL

MRPL is among the more useful refining-focused comparisons because both companies are more exposed to refining economics than fully integrated marketing companies.

### CPCL vs Reliance Industries

Reliance provides large-scale private refining/petrochemicals context, but its diversified business mix, scale, petrochemicals, retail and digital businesses mean it should not mechanically anchor CPCL's valuation.
