## 1. Executive summary

Chennai Petroleum Corporation Limited is a refining company and part of the Indian Oil group. CPCL operates in a highly cyclical refining industry where profitability is driven by gross refining margins, crude throughput, refinery utilization, crude/product price movements, inventory gains or losses, exchange rates, and operating efficiency.

CPCL is different from the previous company cases because it is not primarily a brand, specialty pharma, or mining-cycle story. It is a refining-margin cycle story. The main valuation question is whether recent strong refining margins are sustainable or whether they should normalize over the forecast period.

The public-source v1 thesis is that CPCL should be modelled as a **refining-cycle company with strong recent profitability, high operating leverage to GRMs, IOCL parentage, and meaningful volatility in earnings and working capital**.

The DCF should not treat a strong GRM quarter as permanent. A mid-cycle GRM and normalized-margin approach is more appropriate.

## 4. Investment thesis

CPCL's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Strong refining-margin recovery | Q3 FY26 and 9M FY26 showed a sharp improvement in GRM and profitability. |
| High operating leverage | Small changes in GRM can materially change PAT and cash flow. |
| IOCL parentage | Strategic support and integration benefits, but also capital-allocation and policy considerations. |
| Domestic fuel demand | Indian fuel consumption supports throughput and product demand. |
| Cyclical risk | Valuation must normalize GRMs and avoid extrapolating peak spreads. |

The base case should use normalized refining-margin assumptions. The bull case can capture stronger GRMs, high utilization, lower crude volatility and better product cracks. The bear case should capture margin compression, inventory losses and weaker throughput.

## 6. Financial performance review

CPCL reported a sharp Q3 FY26 turnaround. Public result coverage reported Q3 FY26 revenue from operations of around ₹19,438 crore versus ₹15,683 crore in Q3 FY25. Profit after tax was around ₹987 crore versus around ₹10 crore in Q3 FY25.

The improvement was driven by a strong recovery in gross refining margins. CPCL's GRM for April–December 2025 was reported at around US$7.72/bbl versus US$3.40/bbl for the comparable prior-year period. Q3 FY26 crude throughput was also strong, with capacity utilization above 100%.

The correct research treatment is:

> CPCL's recent profitability is real, but the DCF must normalize refining margins because GRMs can reverse sharply.

## 8. Scenario logic

### Bear case

- GRMs compress sharply
- Inventory losses occur
- Crude/product price volatility increases
- Throughput is affected by maintenance or operational issues
- Capex burden rises
- Working capital absorbs cash

### Base case

- FY26 remains strong but margins normalize
- Throughput remains healthy
- GRM fades toward mid-cycle level
- Capex remains manageable
- Working capital remains volatile but not structurally negative

### Bull case

- GRMs remain elevated
- Product cracks stay favourable
- Refinery utilization stays above 100%
- Inventory losses are limited
- Operational efficiency improves
- Capex drives better product yield

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Refining-Cycle Risk Premium
```

A refining-cycle risk premium is useful because CPCL's historical beta may not fully capture GRM volatility, inventory risk, policy exposure and working-capital swings.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

CPCL's DCF is most sensitive to:

- GRM assumption
- normalized EBITDA / EBIT margin
- throughput utilization
- working-capital assumptions
- WACC
- capex intensity
