# CPCL Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Chennai Petroleum Corporation Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

CPCL is a cyclical refining company. It should not be valued by simply annualizing one strong GRM quarter.

Every assumption should consider:

- gross refining margin cycle
- crude throughput
- refinery utilization
- product cracks
- crude and product inventory gains/losses
- crude price volatility
- USD/INR movement
- refinery maintenance shutdowns
- capex intensity
- working-capital volatility
- IOCL parentage and policy context

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 run-rate plus a normalized Q4 estimate.

### Why

Refining revenue is driven by crude and product prices as well as throughput. Revenue growth does not always mean improved profitability.

### Modelling rule

Use revenue carefully. For refiners, GRM and operating margin matter more than top-line growth alone.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate normalized growth after a strong FY26 base.

### Why

Long-term growth depends on throughput, domestic demand and product prices. Crude/product price changes can inflate or reduce revenue without changing economic profitability.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use normalized margins below strong-quarter levels.

### Why

Refining margins are cyclical. GRMs can improve sharply but can also reverse quickly due to supply/demand changes, crude spreads and product cracks.

### Modelling rule

GRM upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary due to profitability swings, deferred taxes, one-off items and accounting treatment.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with audited annual report and latest quarterly filing.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to refinery asset base and capex.

### Why

Refineries are capital-intensive assets with large plant, machinery and maintenance requirements.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate-to-high capex intensity.

### Why

Refinery maintenance, fuel-quality compliance, safety, environmental requirements and efficiency upgrades require capital.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital normalization.

### Why

Crude and product inventory values can cause large working-capital swings. A company can report profits while free cash flow is pressured by inventory and receivables.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Refining is a mature, cyclical business. Long-term value depends more on mid-cycle GRMs, utilization and capital discipline than aggressive perpetual growth.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Energy / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- refining-cycle risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include a refining-cycle risk overlay because reported beta may not capture full GRM, inventory and working-capital risk.

## Scenario assumption summary

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue / throughput | Lower or flat | Normalized | Strong throughput / price support |
| GRM / margin | Sharp compression | Mid-cycle normalization | Elevated but not permanent |
| Capex | Higher burden | Moderate to high | Productive upgrade capex |
| Working capital | Cash absorption | Normalized | Better cycle management |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Moderate | Slightly higher but disciplined |
| WACC | Higher | Normalized with refining risk | Lower / stable |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for CPCL is refining-cycle normalization: GRM, inventory gains/losses and throughput must be separated from sustainable earnings power.
