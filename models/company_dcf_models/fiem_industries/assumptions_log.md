# Fiem Industries Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Fiem Industries Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Fiem Industries is an auto-components company with strong two-wheeler exposure and meaningful customer concentration. It should not be valued by simply extrapolating one strong quarter.

Every assumption should consider:

- two-wheeler production cycle
- OEM customer concentration
- LED and premium lighting content
- EV two-wheeler optionality
- raw material costs
- operating leverage
- capex and tooling requirements
- working-capital cycle
- replacement market contribution

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus normalized Q4.

### Why

9M FY26 and Q3 FY26 show strong growth, but full-year data and segment mix require audited reconciliation.

### Modelling rule

Use two-wheeler recovery as a growth driver, but do not assume unusually strong growth continues indefinitely.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use moderate growth that fades over time.

### Why

Two-wheeler demand, LED penetration and customer programs support growth, but auto cycles and OEM schedules are volatile.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use sustainable margins near the improved range, but below aggressive bull-case levels.

### Why

Fiem has shown margin improvement, but margins can be affected by raw material costs, customer pricing, product mix and operating leverage.

### Modelling rule

Margin upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary due to one-off items, incentives and accounting treatment.

### Refresh requirement

Reconcile with audited annual report and latest quarterly filing.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to manufacturing asset base, tooling and capex.

### Why

Auto-component manufacturing requires plants, moulds, tools, machinery and quality systems.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate capex intensity.

### Why

Capex is needed for capacity, tooling, lighting technology, R&D, moulds and customer-specific programs.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital assumptions.

### Why

OEM receivables, inventories, production schedules and supplier terms can affect cash conversion.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Auto components can grow with vehicle production and content per vehicle, but customer concentration and cyclical demand prevent aggressive perpetual assumptions.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Auto / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- auto-component / customer-concentration risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include an auto-component and customer-concentration risk overlay because reported beta may not capture OEM dependence and platform concentration risk.

## Scenario assumption summary

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Weak two-wheeler cycle | Moderate growth | Strong cycle and content growth |
| Margin | Compression | Sustainable improved margin | Operating leverage and mix expansion |
| Capex | Higher burden | Moderate | Productive technology/tooling capex |
| Working capital | Cash absorption | Normalized | Better cash conversion |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Moderate | Slightly higher but disciplined |
| WACC | Higher | Normalized with customer risk | Lower / stable |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for Fiem is separating sustainable operating improvement from cycle-driven margin expansion.
