# Fiem Industries Peer Framework v1

## Purpose

This file completes the peer-comparison layer for the Fiem Industries public-source v1 research pack.

Fiem should not be compared mechanically with every auto-component company because its product mix is heavily linked to automotive lighting, mirrors and two-wheeler OEM customers. Peer comparison should be used as a valuation sanity check, not as a replacement for normalized DCF and customer-concentration analysis.

## Interpretation framework

### Fiem vs Lumax Industries

Lumax is the closest lighting peer. It is useful for comparing lighting margins, OEM exposure and valuation, but differences in customer mix and product portfolio still matter.

### Fiem vs Minda Corporation and Uno Minda

Minda Corporation and Uno Minda provide broader auto-component context. Their scale and diversification may justify different valuation multiples.

### Fiem vs Varroc Engineering

Varroc is useful for lighting and component comparison, but leverage, global exposure and business complexity differ from Fiem.

### Fiem vs Endurance and Suprajit

Endurance and Suprajit help frame quality auto-component valuation, but product mix and customer exposures are different.

## Public-source v1 conclusion

The peer framework is complete for v1. The final numerical peer table should be refreshed using one consistent data source and one consistent date.

For this project, peer valuation supports the DCF as a sanity check. It should not override normalized DCF logic because Fiem's free cash flow and margins depend heavily on two-wheeler production, OEM schedules and customer concentration.
