## 1. Executive summary

Fiem Industries is an Indian auto-components company focused mainly on automotive lighting, signalling equipment, rear-view mirrors and related components. Its business is strongly linked to two-wheeler production, OEM customer relationships, LED lighting penetration, product mix, operating leverage and replacement market demand.

Fiem is different from the earlier commodity-cycle cases because it is primarily an **auto-component growth and execution case**. The main valuation debate is whether recent margin strength and two-wheeler recovery can be sustained while managing customer concentration and industry-cycle risk.

The public-source v1 thesis is that Fiem Industries should be modelled as a **two-wheeler-linked auto-components compounder with strong recent execution, improving margins, customer concentration risk, EV/LED optionality and operating-leverage sensitivity**.

The DCF should not simply extrapolate one strong margin quarter forever. A normalized margin and volume-growth approach is more appropriate.

## 4. Investment thesis

Fiem's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Two-wheeler recovery | Higher two-wheeler production supports revenue growth. |
| Product content growth | LED lighting and premiumization can increase content per vehicle. |
| Margin improvement | Q3 FY26 showed margin expansion and operating efficiency. |
| Customer relationships | Large OEM relationships support scale but create concentration risk. |
| EV / technology optionality | EV and advanced lighting can support long-term product relevance. |

The base case should use steady revenue growth and sustainable margins. The bull case can capture stronger two-wheeler production, LED content, EV components and operating leverage. The bear case should reflect OEM slowdown, customer concentration risk, margin compression and raw-material volatility.

## 6. Financial performance review

Fiem Industries reported strong Q3 FY26 performance. Public result coverage showed Q3 FY26 total income of around ₹695.01 crore, up 16.4% year-on-year, and Q3 EPS of ₹24.08. Other result commentary reported Q3 FY26 net sales around ₹685.81 crore, up 16.22% year-on-year, and PAT around ₹63.45 crore, up 33.83% year-on-year.

The company also reported 9M FY26 total sales of around INR 20,475 million and PAT margin of around 9.01%. The 3QFY26 investor presentation indicated that the automotive segment accounted for around 99.80% of sales, and two-wheeler customers represented around 97.32% of automotive segment revenue. TVS Motor and Honda Two Wheeler Group were major customers in Q3 FY26.

The correct research treatment is:

> Fiem's Q3 FY26 execution is strong, but the DCF must normalize margin assumptions and explicitly account for two-wheeler dependence and customer concentration.

## 8. Scenario logic

### Bear case

- Two-wheeler production slows
- Key OEM customer volumes weaken
- LED/content growth disappoints
- Raw material costs rise faster than customer escalations
- Margins revert below recent levels
- Working capital absorbs cash

### Base case

- Two-wheeler demand remains healthy
- Fiem sustains improved operating efficiency
- LED/content growth supports moderate revenue growth
- Margins remain healthy but below aggressive bull levels
- Capex remains manageable

### Bull case

- Two-wheeler cycle remains strong
- LED/premium content per vehicle rises materially
- EV two-wheeler component opportunities scale
- Replacement market improves
- Operating leverage sustains 14%+ EBITDA margins
- Customer diversification improves

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Auto-Component / Customer-Concentration Risk Premium
```

A customer-concentration and auto-cycle risk premium is useful because reported beta may not fully capture OEM dependence, two-wheeler cycle risk, platform concentration and margin pass-through risk.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

Fiem's DCF is most sensitive to:

- two-wheeler production growth
- EBITDA margin sustainability
- customer concentration
- working-capital cycle
- capex / tooling intensity
- WACC
- terminal growth
