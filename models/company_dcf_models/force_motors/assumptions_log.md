# Force Motors Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Force Motors Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Force Motors is an auto/OEM and high-tech aggregate manufacturing company with cyclical vehicle demand and program/customer concentration. It should not be valued by simply extrapolating one strong quarter or exceptional gains.

Every assumption should consider:

- Traveller and Urbania demand
- commercial and passenger carrier cycle
- engine / aggregate manufacturing programs
- customer and product platform concentration
- raw material and component costs
- operating leverage
- capex and product development
- working-capital cycle
- exceptional item normalization
- balance-sheet strength

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus normalized Q4.

### Why

9M FY26 shows strong growth and profitability, but full-year audited data and segment mix require reconciliation.

### Modelling rule

Use Q3/9M momentum as an input, but do not treat exceptional items or peak-cycle margins as recurring earnings power.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use moderate growth that fades over time.

### Why

Traveller, Urbania, specialised mobility and aggregate manufacturing support growth, but auto cycles and product-platform demand are volatile.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use sustainable margins below exceptional or peak-quarter levels.

### Why

Q3 FY26 EBITDA growth was strong, but margins can be affected by raw material costs, mix, volumes, pricing and operating leverage.

### Modelling rule

Margin upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Exceptional items

### Base-case logic

Exclude exceptional gains from normalized operating valuation.

### Why

DCF should value recurring operating cash flows. Exceptional items can support reported PAT but should not be capitalized as recurring earnings.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Tax can be affected by deferred tax, regime changes, exceptional items and one-off accounting effects.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Link depreciation to manufacturing assets, tooling, product platforms and capex.

### Why

Vehicle and aggregate manufacturing requires plants, equipment, tooling, R&D and product development.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use moderate-to-high capex intensity.

### Why

Capex is needed for product programs, safety/emission compliance, platform updates, capacity and high-tech aggregate manufacturing.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital assumptions.

### Why

Vehicles, components, receivables, inventories and supplier schedules can affect cash conversion.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative terminal growth.

### Why

Auto/OEM businesses can grow, but cyclicality, product-platform risk and capex needs prevent aggressive perpetual assumptions.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Auto / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- auto/OEM execution risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include an auto/OEM execution risk overlay because reported beta may not capture platform concentration, program concentration and margin normalization risk.

## Scenario assumption summary

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Weak auto cycle | Moderate growth | Strong platform and aggregate growth |
| Margin | Sharp normalization | Sustainable healthy margin | Operating leverage and mix expansion |
| Capex | Higher burden | Moderate to high | Productive program capex |
| Working capital | Cash absorption | Normalized | Better cash conversion |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Moderate | Slightly higher but disciplined |
| WACC | Higher | Normalized with auto/OEM risk | Lower / stable |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for Force Motors is separating recurring operating improvement from exceptional gains and auto-cycle margin peaks.
