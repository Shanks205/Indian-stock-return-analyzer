# Force Motors Peer Framework v1

## Purpose

This file completes the peer-comparison layer for the Force Motors public-source v1 research pack.

Force Motors should not be compared mechanically with every automobile company because its mix includes vans, utility vehicles, specialised mobility products and high-tech aggregate / engine manufacturing. Peer comparison should be used as a valuation sanity check, not as a replacement for normalized DCF and recurring earnings analysis.

## Interpretation framework

### Force Motors vs Ashok Leyland

Ashok Leyland is useful for commercial vehicle cycle context, but Force Motors has a more specialised product mix and aggregate manufacturing exposure.

### Force Motors vs Tata Motors

Tata Motors is a broad auto reference. It is useful for sector sentiment but should not mechanically anchor Force Motors because of size, product mix and global exposure differences.

### Force Motors vs Mahindra & Mahindra

M&M provides utility vehicle and Indian auto demand context, but its diversified auto, farm and services exposure makes it an imperfect direct peer.

### Force Motors vs SML Isuzu and Atul Auto

These companies help frame smaller vehicle manufacturer valuation and demand cycles, but product positioning and scale differences should be considered.
