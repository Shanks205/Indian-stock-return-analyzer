## 1. Executive summary

Natco Pharma is a vertically integrated pharmaceutical company focused on complex generics, oncology, specialty products, active pharmaceutical ingredients, export formulations, domestic formulations, and crop health sciences.

Unlike a stable consumer or auto business, Natco's earnings profile is naturally lumpy. Its profitability can move sharply depending on high-value product launches, exclusivity periods, regulatory approvals, export formulation performance, and one-off profit-share opportunities.

This makes Natco a good valuation case for the project because it forces the analyst to separate:

- reported high-margin periods from normalized earnings
- one-off product opportunities from recurring base business
- balance-sheet strength from operating volatility
- pipeline optionality from base-case DCF assumptions

The public-source v1 conclusion is that Natco should be modelled as a **net-cash, niche pharmaceutical company with high optionality but volatile normalized earnings**.

## 4. Investment thesis

Natco's investment thesis is based on five pillars:

| Pillar | Research view |
|---|---|
| Niche product strategy | Natco targets complex and high-value opportunities rather than plain commoditized generics. |
| Net-cash balance sheet | Cash strength provides downside support and strategic flexibility. |
| Pipeline optionality | Semaglutide, oncology, and other high-value launches can support upside. |
| Export volatility | Export formulations can create large earnings swings. |
| Normalization discipline | DCF should avoid valuing one-off profit peaks as permanent earnings. |

The base case should normalize Natco's earnings after high-margin product cycles. The bull case can include stronger high-value launches. The bear case should reflect price erosion, regulatory issues, or weak export profitability.

## 6. Financial performance review

Natco's FY25 annual report showed strong full-year performance, including total revenue of INR 47,840 million, EBITDA of INR 25,505 million, EBITDA margin of 53.3%, PAT of INR 18,834 million, ROE of 24.7%, and ROCE of 30.1%.

However, FY26 quarterly performance has been more volatile. Q2 FY26 was strong, helped by export formulations, while Q3 FY26 showed a sharp sequential reduction from Q2 levels. Q3 FY26 total revenue was reported around INR 7,054 million, EBITDA around INR 2,168 million including other income, and PAT around INR 1,513 million.

The correct research treatment is:

> Natco's high-margin FY25 performance should not be treated as a permanent run-rate. The model should normalize earnings and separately discuss pipeline upside.

## 7. DCF assumption justification

| DCF input | Base-case treatment | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue base | Normalized FY26E / FY27E base | FY25 was unusually strong; FY26/FY27 should reflect post-peak normalization. |
| Revenue growth | Recovery after FY27 reset | Growth should reflect domestic formulations, exports ex-major one-offs, and pipeline launches. |
| EBITDA / EBIT margin | Normalized below FY25 peak | FY25 margin was exceptionally high; long-run margin should be more conservative. |
| Depreciation | Linked to revenue and asset intensity | Pharma manufacturing has meaningful plant and compliance assets. |
| Capex | Moderate to high | Required for manufacturing, R&D, filings, compliance, and pipeline execution. |
| Working capital | Conservative | Pharma inventories, receivables, and launch timing can create working-capital swings. |
| Tax rate | Normalized effective tax rate | Needs annual-report and quarterly reconciliation. |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Mature normalized pharma base should not be valued like permanent high-growth optionality. |
| Net cash | Added separately to equity value | Net cash is meaningful and should be treated explicitly. |

## 8. Scenario logic

### Bear case

- Product concentration weakens
- US pricing erosion is severe
- Semaglutide and pipeline launches disappoint
- Regulatory issues delay approvals
- Margins normalize faster than expected
- Associate investment contributes less than expected

### Base case

- FY25 peak normalizes

- Domestic formulations grow steadily
- Export business remains volatile but profitable
- Net cash supports equity value
- Pipeline optionality is included conservatively

### Bull case

- Semaglutide and oncology launches outperform

- Adcock investment contributes meaningfully
- Crop Health restructuring unlocks value
- Margins stabilize at a higher-than-base level

## 9. WACC framework

### Cost of equity

```text
Cost of Equity = Risk-free Rate + Beta × Equity Risk Premium + Company-Specific Risk Premium
```

A company-specific risk premium is appropriate because a low observed beta may understate fundamental risks such as product concentration, regulatory events, price erosion, and launch dependence.

### WACC

```text
WACC = Equity Weight × Cost of Equity + Debt Weight × After-tax Cost of Debt
```

Natco has a strong net-cash profile, so the DCF is primarily sensitive to cost of equity, margin normalization, terminal growth, and pipeline assumptions.
