# Triveni Turbine Assumptions Log

## Research version

**Version:** Public-Source Complete v1 — Institutional-Style Preliminary Valuation  
**Company:** Triveni Turbine Limited  
**Valuation date:** 29 April 2026  
**Purpose:** Document the logic behind major valuation assumptions.

---

## Assumption discipline

Triveni Turbine is a capital-goods and industrial equipment company. It should not be valued by simply extrapolating one strong quarter or one period of strong order inflow.

Every assumption should consider:

- order book and order inflow
- export growth
- domestic industrial capex
- aftermarket/service contribution
- margin sustainability
- execution timing
- working-capital cycle
- currency movement
- competition and pricing
- product engineering and capacity needs

---

## Revenue base

### Base-case logic

Use FY26E based on 9M FY26 performance plus normalized Q4.

### Why

9M FY26 and Q3 FY26 show strong execution, but full-year audited data and order-book conversion require reconciliation.

### Modelling rule

Use order-book strength as revenue visibility, but do not treat every strong order-inflow period as permanent growth.

---

## Revenue growth

### Base-case logic

Use healthy growth that fades over time.

### Why

Exports, order book and aftermarket support growth, but capital-goods orders can be lumpy and cyclical.

---

## EBITDA / EBIT margin

### Base-case logic

Use sustainable high margins, but below aggressive bull-case levels.

### Why

Q3 FY26 EBITDA margin was strong, but margins can be affected by product mix, export mix, execution efficiency, currency and competition.

### Modelling rule

Margin upside should be stronger in the bull case, not fully embedded in the base case.

---

## Tax rate

### Base-case logic

Use a normalized effective tax rate.

### Why

Actual tax can vary due to one-off items, incentives and accounting treatment.

---

## Depreciation and amortization

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate depreciation intensity.

### Why

Triveni Turbine is engineering-led and less asset-heavy than commodity or heavy manufacturing businesses, but still requires equipment, facilities and product development.

---

## Capital expenditure

### Base-case logic

Use low-to-moderate capex intensity.

### Why

Capex is needed for capacity, product engineering, testing, service infrastructure and export capability.

---

## Working capital

### Base-case logic

Use conservative working-capital assumptions.

### Why

Capital-goods companies can have receivables, inventories, advances and execution-linked working-capital swings.

---

## Terminal growth

### Base-case logic

Use conservative-to-moderate terminal growth.

### Why

Exports and energy-transition applications support growth, but order cyclicality and competition prevent aggressive perpetual assumptions.

---

## WACC

### Required inputs

- India 10-year government bond yield
- regression beta vs Nifty Capital Goods / Nifty 50
- India equity risk premium
- capital-goods / export execution risk premium
- cost of debt
- effective tax rate
- market value of equity
- cash and debt balances

### Current status

Framework complete for v1. Numerical WACC should include a capital-goods / export execution risk overlay because reported beta may not fully capture order-book cyclicality, export execution risk and margin normalization risk.

## Scenario assumption summary

| Assumption | Bear case | Base case | Bull case |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue growth | Weak order inflow | Healthy but fading growth | Strong order book and export growth |
| Margin | Compression | Sustainable high margin | Aftermarket and export mix expansion |
| Capex | Higher burden | Low-to-moderate | Productive engineering/service capex |
| Working capital | Cash absorption | Normalized | Better cash conversion |
| Terminal growth | Conservative | Moderate | Slightly higher but disciplined |
| WACC | Higher | Normalized with execution risk | Lower / stable |

## Final note

This assumptions log should be updated whenever the DCF model changes. The key analytical discipline for Triveni Turbine is separating sustainable order-book and aftermarket quality from one-off margin or order-inflow spikes.
