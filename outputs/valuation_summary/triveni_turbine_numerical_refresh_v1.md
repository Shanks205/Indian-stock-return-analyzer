## Why the model normalizes Triveni Turbine

Triveni Turbine's Q3 FY26 performance shows strong execution, export momentum and high margins. However, capital-goods order inflow, order conversion, product mix and export growth can be lumpy.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4
- healthy growth that fades over time
- sustainable high margins but below aggressive bull-case levels
- conservative working-capital treatment
- low-to-moderate capex intensity
- capital-goods / export execution risk premium in cost of equity

## Preliminary interpretation

The public-source v1 DCF should be interpreted as a normalized capital-goods growth model, not a forecast of one strong quarter continuing permanently.

For Triveni Turbine, the biggest valuation debate is whether high margins and order-book strength can convert into durable free cash flow without working-capital strain or order-inflow normalization.
