## Why the model normalizes Force Motors

Force Motors' Q3/9M FY26 performance shows strong operating momentum and reported PAT after exceptional items. However, a professional DCF should not capitalize exceptional gains or one strong auto-cycle period as permanent earnings power.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4
- normalized operating profitability using PBT before exceptional items
- moderate growth that fades over time
- sustainable but not overextended margins
- conservative capex and working-capital treatment
- auto/OEM execution risk premium in cost of equity

## Preliminary interpretation

The public-source v1 DCF should be interpreted as a normalized auto/OEM operating model, not a forecast of one strong quarter or exceptional gain continuing permanently.

For Force Motors, the biggest valuation debate is whether strong operating leverage and debt-free positioning can be sustained while auto-cycle and program concentration risks remain.
