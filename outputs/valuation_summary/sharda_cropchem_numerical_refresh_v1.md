## Why the model normalizes Sharda Cropchem

Sharda Cropchem's Q3 FY26 performance shows strong recovery in revenue, EBITDA and PAT. However, agrochemical demand, product mix, channel inventory and working-capital behaviour can be volatile. A professional DCF should therefore avoid treating one strong recovery quarter as permanent.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4
- moderate recovery growth that fades over time
- normalized margins below an aggressive bull-case level
- conservative working-capital treatment
- low-to-moderate capex due to asset-light structure
- agrochemical / working-capital risk premium in cost of equity

## Preliminary interpretation

The public-source v1 DCF should be interpreted as a normalized agrochemical recovery model, not a forecast of one strong quarter continuing permanently.

For Sharda Cropchem, the biggest valuation debate is not only revenue growth. The bigger question is whether EBITDA recovery converts into free cash flow after inventory and receivable requirements.
