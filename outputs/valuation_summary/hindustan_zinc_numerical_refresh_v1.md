## Why the model normalizes Hindustan Zinc heavily

Hindustan Zinc's current profitability is strong, helped by zinc and silver prices. However, zinc, lead and silver are cyclical commodities. A professional DCF should therefore not assume the current metal-price environment continues forever.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on recent quarterly run-rate
- margin fade from strong current levels to mid-cycle levels
- moderate growth after a strong base
- moderate-to-high capex intensity
- conservative terminal growth
- commodity / ownership risk premium in cost of equity

## Interpretation

The conservative public-source v1 DCF does not fully justify the current spot price under normalized zinc/silver cycle assumptions. This does not mean the company is weak. It means the market price appears to be discounting strong metal prices, silver contribution, dividend appeal and sustained high margins.

For Hindustan Zinc, the biggest valuation debate is not whether current results are strong. The biggest debate is what level of zinc/silver pricing and EBITDA margin should be treated as sustainable mid-cycle profitability.
