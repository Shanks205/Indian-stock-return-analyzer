## Why the model normalizes Time Technoplast

Time Technoplast's Q3/9M FY26 performance supports the value-added product thesis. However, industrial demand, raw-material pricing, working capital and capex requirements can create free-cash-flow volatility.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4
- moderate growth that fades over time
- sustainable but not overextended margins
- conservative working-capital treatment
- moderate capex intensity
- industrial / working-capital risk premium in cost of equity

## Preliminary interpretation

The public-source v1 DCF should be interpreted as a normalized industrial packaging and value-added polymer-products growth model, not a forecast of one strong quarter continuing permanently.

For Time Technoplast, the biggest valuation debate is whether value-added product growth can translate into durable free cash flow after working-capital and capex needs.
