## Why the model normalizes Fiem Industries

Fiem's Q3 FY26 performance shows strong revenue and profit growth. However, the company has high dependence on two-wheeler OEM production, customer programs and product mix. A professional DCF should therefore avoid treating one strong margin period as permanent.

The model therefore uses:

- FY26E revenue based on 9M FY26 plus normalized Q4
- moderate growth that fades over time
- sustainable but not overextended margins
- conservative working-capital treatment
- moderate capex/tooling intensity
- auto-component / customer-concentration risk premium in cost of equity

## Preliminary interpretation

The public-source v1 DCF should be interpreted as a normalized auto-component growth model, not a forecast of one strong quarter continuing permanently.

For Fiem, the biggest valuation debate is whether improved margins can be sustained while customer concentration and two-wheeler cycle risk remain high.
